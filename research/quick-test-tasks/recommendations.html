Why do skeptic verdicts override the raw scores?

ordering

The mechanical sort is by rubric total (28 or 27 of 28), which puts c05 (FSM/state tracking) in the top tier. This doc demotes c05 out of the recommended set and promotes c03 (total 27) to #2:

  • c05 — refuted by both lenses (major). Its latent rules (AUTH-before-DATA, undefined-transition fallback, monotone sequence numbers) are textbook protocol conventions a competent prompt engineer states one-shot. Its difficulty knob (trace length) is caught in a dilemma: long traces make the ceiling unreachable in a single no-scratchpad call (its own citations — arXiv:2503.02854, arXiv:2509.09677 — document this), while short traces plus CoT leave a 2-3 rung ladder that one demo bootstrap collapses. The fixes amount to redesigning it into c21/c03. The 28/28 scorecard did not price this in.
  • c15 and c01 — refuted by both lenses (major). c15's rule family is famous flipped-label literature, so a "here are the rule types, induce the bindings from demos" meta-prompt one-shots it. c01 is trapped between explicit specs (one-shot) and implicit quirks (no writable ~100% reference prompt), and its encode direction has non-unique ground truth. Both excluded.
  • c03 — promoted. Scored 27 (criterion 4 = 1) but is the only candidate besides c21 where neither skeptic refuted the design — both lenses concluded the incremental ladder survives, contingent on concrete, cheap fixes. A candidate whose weakest criterion survived adversarial attack outranks 28-scorers whose measurement story did not.

Remaining order weighs verdict health first, then tie-breaker strength, then adaptation cost.

1 · c21 — Relational Micro-World with Invented Vocabulary

adopt Relational/kinship QA over nonce graphs · rubric 28/28 · both lenses not refuted, minor severity only — the only finalist with no major finding.

The task: nonce relations with seeded nonstandard properties

A pinned generator builds a small graph of nonce entities connected by nonce relation symbols. Each relation symbol carries seeded, nonstandard properties fixed per generator seed: symmetry, transitivity, an inverse mapping, a two-relation composition table, explicit-negation precedence, and closed-world handling. Answers use an invented closed vocabulary (a gender-neutral "nieph", a "bond-" prefix for in-law-style links, a relation-distance cutoff).

An instance is a few facts plus one query; output is one/two words from the closed vocabulary or a 4-way MC letter (true / false / both / unknown), scored 0/1 exact match against a graph-traversal oracle.

Example instance

This seed: tovv is transitive, non-symmetric; two tovv hops map to codeword nieph; brell is symmetric; closed-world default for tovv is unknown.

Facts:
  Marn tovv Belu.
  Belu tovv Sagre.
  NOT (Sagre brell Marn).

Q1: What is Sagre to Marn? (one word, task vocabulary)
Gold: nieph   — two tovv-hops.
Naive prompt: "grandchild" → 0.

Q2: Does Belu tovv Marn? A true / B false / C both / D unknown
Gold: D   — tovv not symmetric; closed-world default unknown.
Naive prompt guessing symmetry: A → 0.

Scorecard: straight 2s, and the floor is structural

  • Criterion 4 is structural, not tuned: the hidden rulebook is arbitrary seeded information — no zero-shot prompt can contain the per-seed property assignments or the codebook, and the rules are under-determined from any single instance, so "infer the rules yourself" prompts cannot shortcut the ladder.
  • ~9 independent strata (six relation-property rules + three vocabulary rules), each worth roughly 10-15 points of pool score (criterion 10).
  • Diagnostic failures: codebook misses (standard English kin term emitted) separate cleanly from traversal misses; within traversal, the violated rule is identifiable from expected-vs-got (criterion 11).

Weak spots (from the verdicts; none refuting)

  • A best-effort "uninterpreted-symbols, closed-world literal logician" prompt could land at 40-60% if hidden-rule strata are under-weighted.
  • Some rules (composition table, distance cutoff) may be practically non-inducible from 0/1 feedback, stalling all optimizers at the same sub-ceiling.
  • Multiple-valid-answer instances and an incoherent "both" label are latent generator bugs.
  • Uniform random 10-task minibatches have a ~21% chance of missing a given stratum entirely.

Surviving skeptic caveats → mitigations already in the plan

Adaptation plan

Build the two pinned artifacts

A graph sampler (nonce entities + per-seed relation property profiles) and a traversal oracle computing exact answers. Pin generator version, dataset manifest, and disjoint minibatch/subset/official split identities.

Cap hop depth and balance answers

Full-rule reference prompt must reach ~100%; balance the four MC outcomes and closed-vocab answers so constant-guess baselines are pinned low (25% MC floor).

Tag every instance with its latent-rule stratum

For failure attribution and stratified aggregation (average 0/1 within, then across strata).

Store baselines and pre-register the success criterion

Reference ~100% prompt, naive baseline, literal-logician baseline; e.g. recover ≥90% of the gap within 8 iterations — set only after the discoverability check confirms every retained rule is inducible.

Normalize answers

Lowercase, strip whitespace, canonicalize hyphenated bond- terms; unparseable/off-vocabulary/empty outputs are decisive 0s (exercises criterion 13).

Add the second objective

Mean output UTF-8 bytes as the distinct second objective with a fixed length-budget axis, per the rubric's two-objective callout.

Tie-breaker: strong (2/2). Sits squarely in the active in-context rule-learning / synthetic-formal-language ICL lines (cipher-ICL, GINC/RegBench, MIR-Bench, FalsifyBench, the LINGOLY/Linguini linguistics-olympiad family), directly adjacent to PrOntoQA's fictional-symbol methodology (ICLR 2023). The invented-vocabulary + seeded-property design is exactly the contamination-proof, decomposable, few-shot-sensitive probe those 2025-2026 lines care about; it could grow into a standalone optimizer-comparison benchmark. Only gap: no flagship live leaderboard to anchor to.

2 · c03 — Keyed Custom-Operator Expression Evaluation

backup Invented operators / nonstandard precedence arithmetic · rubric 27/28 (criterion 4 = 1) · both lenses not refuted (major-severity caveats with concrete fixes) — the only other candidate left standing by both skeptics.

The task: per-instance symbol tables, family-constant hidden conventions

Each instance opens with a per-instance symbol table defining invented infix operators as closed forms (e.g. (a·k + b) mod m), decoy symbols, an opaque mode token, and a short chained expression. Output is one integer (or a 4-way MC letter), scored 0/1 exact match against a ~10-line reference evaluator.

Latent, family-constant conventions carry the headroom: modular wrap into residues 1..m (not 0..m-1), the opaque mode token's semantics (precedence/associativity), threshold post-processing, and a recurrence stratum with an index term.

Example instance

Family-latent conventions: ⊞ binds tighter than ◇; results wrap into 1..m.

Definitions: x ◇ y = (3x + 2y) mod 6
             x ⊞ y = |x − y| + 1
             x ⨂ y = x        (decoy, never used)
             mode: K3
Evaluate: 2 ◇ 4 ⊞ 3

Gold: 4   — house precedence: 4 ⊞ 3 = 2, then 2 ◇ 2 = 10 mod 6 = 4.
Naive PEMDAS/left-to-right: 2 ◇ 4 = 2, then 2 ⊞ 3 = 2 → answers 2 → 0.

Highlights

  • Textbook single-call + exact-integer-match graph (criteria 1-3, 14); per-instance random parameters make it the most straightforwardly contamination-proof candidate.
  • The reference evaluator gives an exact, checkable ceiling (criterion 9).
  • Diagnostic error signatures: off-by-one at the residue boundary flags the wrap rule, order errors flag associativity, magnitude errors flag precedence.

Weak spots

  • Criterion 4 scored 1: a generic "ignore PEMDAS, read the table literally, obey the flags, output only the integer" prompt captures everything the instance states legibly. Headroom lives entirely in conventions the instance does not state or actively mis-states. Fixable but load-bearing.
  • Quantified measurement hole (found by simulation in the verdict): naive 0..m-1 and house 1..m answers coincide on 85-97% of unforced instances, so learning the wrap rule would be worth ~2 points on a 15-task eval — far below the ≥10-point resolvability bar.

Surviving skeptic caveats → fixes

  • Legible flags = one-shot rungs. All per-instance rule toggles become opaque tokens (mode: K3) whose semantics are family-global latents never stated in any instance; surface mod m notation stays deliberately misleading vs the 1..m truth.
  • Invisible wrap-rule effect size. Foil-bank rejection sampling: evaluate every candidate instance under wrong-wrap, wrong-associativity, PEMDAS, no-threshold, and no-index foils; accept only instances where each active stratum's foil answer differs from truth (~m extra evaluator calls per instance). MC distractors come from the deduplicated foil outputs.
  • Demo leak. Scope few-shot demos by stratum so no single demo set reveals more than one hidden convention.
  • COPRO scoping (structural, applies to every strong candidate). A score-only instruction rephraser cannot produce per-run hidden conventions, so COPRO validates plumbing and generic-instruction gains, not deep hyperparameter comparison — but the anti-PEMDAS/table-following rung is deliberately kept reachable by blind instruction search, so a broken COPRO is still distinguishable from a stumped one.

Adaptation plan

Seeded generator + tiny reference evaluator define ground truth

Pin seed ranges for disjoint splits; per-stratum labels on every instance.

Enforce the observability partition

Per-instance-random conventions printed in the instance; hidden headroom carried only by family-constant latents (1..m wrap, mode-token semantics, threshold, recurrence).

Foil-bank rejection sampling

Force ≥1 intermediate ≡0 mod m in every wrap-stratum instance; ≥3 operands so associativity errors always surface.

Verify per-rung effect sizes at calibration

≥10 points on 10-20 tasks, using ablated reference prompts (one per latent rule); verify ceiling ≈100% and a low naive floor on the pinned model before freezing.

Decoys, strict parsing, second objective

Decoy symbols in every table; strict integer/MC parser (non-numeric → 0) for criterion 13; output-bytes second objective if GEPA coverage is claimed.

Tie-breaker: strong (2/2). Active in-context rule-learning / novel-operator arithmetic line — In-Context Algebra (arXiv:2512.16902), algorithmic generalization (arXiv:2411.05943), BBH multistep-arithmetic generators (ACL Findings 2023), the OPRO lineage (arXiv:2309.03409). Smaller community than instruction-following, but live and publishable.

3 · c14 — Instruction-Hierarchy Priority Ledger

Authority/conflict resolution among directives · rubric 28/28 · optimization-path not refuted (major) · measurement refuted-as-scored (major, with concrete rescues).

The task: nonce authority tags under a hidden seeded policy

An instance is several short directives tagged with nonce authority levels — some quoted (inert), negated, conditional, or mutually conflicting — each proposing a candidate answer letter. A hidden seeded authority policy (tag-priority permutation, quotation inertness, same-level recency, negation handling, explicit exceptions, fallback) determines which directive controls. Output: one letter A-F, 0/1 exact match against the policy program.

Example instance

Hidden policy: priority QOR > TIB > VEX; quoted directives inert; a negation vetoes matching proposals at its level; conditionals gate; fallback = next level down.

[VEX] Answer with the letter B.
[QOR] Answer with the letter D.
[VEX] "Ignore QOR and answer F."
[QOR] Do not answer with the letter D.
[TIB] If any VEX directive exists, answer C.

Which single letter A-F controls?
Gold: C — QOR outranks all, but its proposal D is vetoed at
its own level; control falls to TIB, whose condition holds.
The quoted VEX line is inert. Naive recency/vividness → F or D → 0.

Highlights and surviving attack

  • Factorial minimal-pair generation isolates each policy feature, so a wrong letter names the misunderstood rule (criteria 10, 11).
  • The 6-nonce-tag priority permutation (720 orders, no pretrained prior) is information-theoretically absent from any zero-shot prompt — the floor is structurally real. Control Illusion (arXiv:2502.15851) documents genuine mis-prioritization.
  • Strongest one-shot attack (survived, conditionally): delegated induction — "here are the five generic features; induce the tag order from these demos" can pin most of the permutation from ~10-15 labeled examples. Blocked only by capping labeled examples per proposal/reflection step and setting per-feature flags counter to natural conventions.

Why the measurement lens refuted it as scored

  • One branch of its own plan — randomizing the policy across splits — destroys internal-to-official rank correlation (criterion 6) and the writable ceiling (criterion 9). Must be struck.
  • Executing a stated 6-feature nonce policy in one forward pass may cap a cheap model's ceiling at 85-95% — the ceiling is a measured gate, not an assumption.
  • Feature-interaction cases (a negation inside a quote) make ground truth implementation-dependent unless an application order is pinned.
  • RLHF priors (later-wins, quoted-text-is-mention) can make some strata dead (floor ≈ ceiling).
  • The proposed second objective is degenerate as stated (every correct output is already one letter).

Adaptation plan (incorporating both skeptics' fixes)

One fixed hidden policy per run, identical across splits

Vary policy only across runs. The policy-stating reference prompt must pass a pre-registered gate — ≥95% at temp 0 on 100+ held-out instances on the pinned cheap model — else shrink directive count/tag alphabet. Record the measured ceiling as the criterion-9 anchor.

Seed anti-prior flag settings

Quotation not always inert, primacy sometimes beating recency; measure per-stratum floors at calibration and drop/reweight dead strata.

Pin the feature-application order

Inertness → negation → exceptions → priority → recency → fallback, in the versioned schema; exclude cross-feature interactions from single-feature strata or give them an explicit interaction stratum.

Throttle demo evidence

Cap demos/reflection minibatch sizes so pairwise-order evidence per step underdetermines the permutation; the calibration adversary is the delegated-induction prompt, not a naive one.

Fix normalization and the second objective

Strip, casefold, must equal one char A-F, else 0. Score correctness on the final line while measuring bytes over the full output, so reasoning-before-answer trades accuracy against bytes.

Tie-breaker: the strongest in the pool. Instruction hierarchy is top-tier, safety-relevant, actively benchmarked 2025-2026 territory: IHEval (arXiv:2502.08745), The Instruction Hierarchy (arXiv:2404.13208), Control Illusion (arXiv:2502.15851). A synthetic nonce-tag priority ledger with factorial minimal pairs would be a novel, contamination-proof contribution with genuine standalone-benchmark potential. If the tie-breaker were weighted equal to the rubric, this candidate would rank #2.

4 · c11 — House-Convention Canonicalizer

Serialization/normalization under invented house rules · rubric 28/28 · optimization-path not refuted (major) · measurement refuted-as-specified (major, rescued by a rule-set change).

The task: one pipeline, 5-6 invented house rules

One deterministic normalization pipeline (canonical JSON or slug preferred) governed by 5-6 invented house rules that deliberately deviate from every public standard. Instance = a messy value; output = the canonical string; 0/1 whole-string exact match against a ~40-line reference function. Each rule owns a stratum, so pool accuracy climbs rule by rule and expected/got diffs name the violated rule.

Directly mirrors the project's dr-serialize canonical-JSON theme, so intuitions transfer to the main experiment.

Example instance

Slug family; house rules: lowercase; separator ~ not -; invented stopword list {the, of, an}; ordinals map through an invented table (2nd → twyx); keep ≤4 tokens, truncating from the end.

Input title: The Quick   Brown Fox (2nd Edition)!

Gold: quick~brown~fox~twyx
Naive slugify: the-quick-brown-fox-2nd-edition → 0.
Each wrong rule (kept "the", used -, missed twyx,
no truncation) is visible in the diff.

Why the measurement lens refuted it as specified

  • The merged rule set included computation-heavy rules — a custom check digit, length-then-codepoint sorts over long key lists, bespoke float formatting — that a small model cannot reliably execute in one constrained pass even with the rules stated verbatim. True ceiling 70-90% and unknowable in advance (criterion 9 fails), with residual slips landing exactly where temp-0 provider nondeterminism flips tokens.
  • The output-bytes second objective is degenerate (correct canonical strings have fixed length); raw whole-completion matching conflates format compliance with rule knowledge.

Optimization-path caveats (survived)

  • The plan's own "demo sets covering all invented month abbreviations, all check-digit cases" manufactures its strongest one-shot attack — full-coverage demos turn lookup rules into copy-from-context.
  • Table-width vs minibatch-width was never pinned, so one GEPA reflection could transcribe every rule visible in a batch.

Adaptation plan (incorporating both skeptics' fixes)

Restrict rules to single-pass-executable transformations

Table lookups (invented month/stopword/ordinal tables), character-class rewrites, fixed replacements, whitespace collapsing, truncation policy, sorts of ≤3 short keys. Drop the arithmetic check digit and long-list sorts (or reduce the check digit to a fully enumerable ≤3-digit lookup). Rule count ≤6.

Enforce table-width > coverage

Table-valued rules get more entries (12-20) than any demo set or GEPA minibatch (cap minibatch ~5; cap demo sets below full table coverage) so rules must accrete over iterations. Strike "cover all cases" from demo curation.

Generator is adversarial per instance

Every instance actively distinguishes house rule from public standard (reject inputs where they coincide); global formatting rules form an explicit first-rung stratum; ≥2 tasks per stratum in every internal subset.

Pre-freeze certification

Reference prompt ≥95% exact match with 3 agreeing temp-0 repeats on the pinned official split; derive floor/gap targets from measured numbers. Freeze exactly one trivial normalization (strip surrounding whitespace/code fences) in the eval config.

Red-team the demo attack; declare the objective limitation

Run the "infer every rule from the examples" one-shot with the largest permitted demo set; treat >50% as a rule-mix bug. Document that the output-bytes objective is degenerate here — this candidate validates single-objective optimization plus two-objective plumbing only (a real limitation vs c21/c14).

Tie-breaker: strong (2/2). Rides the verifiable/constraint-based instruction-following surge and text normalization specifically — PolyNorm (arXiv:2511.03080), StructEval (arXiv:2505.20139), JSONSchemaBench (arXiv:2501.10868), with RFC 8785 JCS as a task donor. Derivative of an active wave rather than opening one — but the dr-serialize alignment is a project-specific bonus no other candidate has.

5 · c12 — Sigil-Schema Field Extraction and Dialect Routing

Structured extraction under invented markups · rubric 28/28 · optimization-path not refuted (major) · measurement refuted-as-specified (major, rescued by stratum redesign).

The task: one field per instance, invented markups

Synthetic records in invented markups — sigil-tagged fields, fixed-width columns, custom-escaped delimiters, or dialects signaled by a header token — where each instance asks for ONE field returned in normalized house form. Output is a single short token, 0/1 exact match against the generator's own record.

Latent rules: sigil→field map, per-dialect conventions, padding/order conventions, escape handling, per-field normalization (randomized implied-decimal position), enum precedence.

Example instance

Dialect VEL: ᚠ marks unit price with implied decimal at 3 places this run; ᚷ marks a date; a second money-shaped field ᚢ is a distractor.

Record: ⟦hdr:VEL⟧ ᚠ7203 ᚢ1150 ᚷ0925
Query: unit price, in house form.

Gold: 7.203 — sigil map picks ᚠ over the same-type
distractor ᚢ; this run's implied-decimal position is 3.
Naive extraction: 7203 or 72.03 → 0.

Why the measurement lens refuted it as specified

  • The checksum VALID/INVALID stratum breaks three criteria at once: 50% guess floor, zero-bit binary failures a reflection LM cannot learn from, and an arithmetic-execution ceiling a cheap model cannot reach.
  • Exact character offsets in fixed-width strata fight BPE tokenization.
  • Cents→decimal at a fixed position is world knowledge, not latent headroom.

Optimization-path caveats (survived)

  • A semantic type-matching prompt ("find the field matching the queried type") solves the sigil stratum for free unless every record carries ≥2 same-type distractors.
  • COPRO's score-only proposer cannot reach per-run randomized rules — scope it to plumbing validation, as with c03/c21.

Adaptation plan (incorporating both skeptics' fixes)

Drop or replace the checksum stratum

Use a non-arithmetic decision rule — e.g. output the field whose stated check char mismatches a stated reference (comparison/lookup, not modular arithmetic).

Redesign fixed-width strata

Field order plus an invented padding glyph (short ≤4-char widths, distinctive pad runs) rather than character counting.

Force distractors; randomize normalization

≥2 same-type distractor fields per record; randomize the implied-decimal position per run (2/3/4 places) so normalization is an inducible rule, not a Stripe convention.

Pre-flight gate per stratum

Spec-dump reference prompt ≥98% per stratum on the pinned model/seed before a stratum enters the pool; record per-stratum reference/floor scores in the manifest so gap-recovery is falsifiable per stratum.

Stratified splits and strict output surface

Even dialect/normalization mixes; canonical single-token output with a published exact-match normalizer; pinned generator + seed metadata.

Tie-breaker: solid, not flagship (2/2 with caveats). EDC (arXiv:2404.03868) canonicalization, StructText (arXiv:2507.21340) as a reverse generator, plus an LLMStructBench-style "prompting beats model size" finding whose provenance is unverified — do not cite it publicly without checking.

Which strong scorers were excluded, and why?

near-misses · exclusions
  • c06 — Artificial-Grammar Violation Classification (28/28). Same verdict profile as c11/c12 (opt-path survives with major caveats, measurement refuted-then-rescued). Excluded from the top five only because its rescue moves headroom from execution difficulty to hidden rule tables — converging on what c21 and c03 already do more cleanly — and its convention-exploiting-prompt floor (~50-60% if instantiations are mnemonic) needs a red-team gate the others don't. A fine sixth choice. Sources: MLRegTest (JMLR 2024), FLaRe (arXiv:2411.07107).
  • c05, c15, c01 — both skeptic lenses refuted each; see the ordering section above.
  • c07 — Case-Marked Micro-Conlang — refuted on the optimization path outright: its gloss table hands the latent morphology to the model in-context, and its "hard rule" (case beats word order) is the highest-prior rule in linguistics; the rescue (MTOB-style withheld glosses) is a structural redesign that converts it into a different candidate.

How to decide among these

One scoping fact, true of all five survivors: any task that is genuinely not one-shot guessable (criterion 4) and contamination-proof (criterion 8) puts the decisive knowledge outside COPRO's score-only instruction rephrasing. On every candidate, COPRO validates contract plumbing and generic-instruction gains; deep hyperparameter ranking comes from MIPROv2/GEPA/agent. Plan for that regardless of choice.

Lowest risk, most rungs → c21

  • Only candidate with no major-severity finding; ~9 independent strata gives the finest-grained optimizer/hyperparameter resolution; all remaining work is verification gates, not redesign.
  • Cost: the graph oracle + rejection sampler is the most intricate generator in the set; the discoverability check adds a pre-pinning step.
  • Choose this unless generator effort is the binding constraint.

Simplest build → c03

  • A ~10-line evaluator and an expression sampler; the foil-bank fix is mechanical.
  • Cost: fewer rungs (~4-5 family-constant latents), coarser hyperparameter resolution; criterion 4 depends entirely on keeping mode-token semantics opaque — one legibility slip collapses a rung.
  • Choose this if you want the quick test running this week.

Best research upside → c14

  • Top-tier, safety-relevant instruction-hierarchy line; strongest standalone publication; the only candidate with a non-degenerate two-objective story after its fix.
  • Cost: heaviest calibration burden (measured ceiling gate, dead-strata audit, anti-prior seeding, demo-leak throttling); its ladder is the most vulnerable to a strong reflection LM naming several features in one step.

Best transfer to the main experiment → c11

  • Canonical-JSON house rules directly rehearse dr-serialize intuitions.
  • Cost: its second objective is provably degenerate (must declare single-objective-plus-plumbing coverage per the rubric callout); needs the rule-set diet plus table-width policing before its ceiling is trustworthy.

Best failure-taxonomy workout → c12

  • Dialect routing + format traps naturally exercise parse-failure paths and the retry machinery.
  • Cost: needed the most stratum surgery (checksum removal, fixed-width redesign); its per-instance single-token failures are the least individually diagnostic for GEPA — diagnosis lives at the batch level.

Recommended path. Build c21; while its generator is under construction, stand up c03 in a day or two as the plumbing smoke test (its evaluator is trivial), then retire c03 or keep it as a second pinned family for cross-checking optimizer rankings. If the team later wants the quick test to grow into its own research direction, c14 is the one to invest the extra calibration effort in.
