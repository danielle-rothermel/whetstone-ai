Rosetta-to-Match-Up MC Linguistic Puzzles ranks #15 of 20 — clean design, high rebuild cost

RANKED #15

A seeded conlang grammar becomes glossed pairs plus a 4-way choice, one rule per option.

whetstone-ai · quick-test-tasks · round-2 rescore

c08 carries the cleanest scoring story in the whole rule-induction cluster: converting Rosetta-style translation into Match-Up multiple choice collapses noisy free-text morphology into a single-letter exact match, and the distractor construction gives a built-in incremental ladder where each captured rule eliminates one wrong answer. The design is admired but not adopted because the implementation floor is high — the only reusable codebase, LINGOLY, is a frozen scoring harness over hand-authored UKLO puzzles with no generator, no sampling, no seed, and no distractor logic, so the load-bearing engineering (a re-seedable conlang generator with latent-rule strata and a rule-based oracle) must be written essentially from scratch. That reuse penalty, priced through a run-verification-capped I1, is why a design this rubric-clean settles in the mid-lower pack, edging just below sibling c09, which salvages more code.

What does it actually ask the model to do?

What it proposes

Match-Up MC turns translation difficulty into single-letter exact match

A generator seeds a fresh conlang grammar per run — nonce roots plus affixes marking word order, agreement, affix meaning, and a diacritic/tone layer with a marker-precedence hierarchy (tone resolves a homograph, a context particle overrides tone). Each instance presents N glossed example pairs plus one held-out query as a 4-way multiple choice asking which pairing or meaning is correct. The distractors are constructed so each differs from the truth by exactly one latent rule, so every rule the model actually induces eliminates one distractor — a built-in incremental ladder with diagnostic wrong answers. Output is a single letter, scored by exact match, and the whole pool is contamination-proof because the grammar is regenerated each run.

Example instance

Illustrative — constructed from the merged summary; a two-rule instance where the SOV word-order rule and the case-suffix agreement rule each isolate one distractor.

Glossed pairs (conlang → English):
  tam-uk  belu-na   →  the child sees the dog
  belu-uk tam-na    →  the dog sees the child
  mira-uk tam-na    →  the bird sees the child

Query: which English meaning matches "tam-uk mira-na"?
  A. the child sees the bird      <- gold
  B. the bird sees the child      (violates: -uk marks subject, not position)
  C. sees the child the bird      (violates: English SVO output order)
  D. the child sees a bird        (violates: article/definiteness rule)

Gold: A
Naive-prompt miss: a model anchoring on surface word order picks B,
reading the first noun as subject and ignoring the -uk/-na case suffixes.

Where does the design come from?

Academic anchor & lineage

Literature parents

  • LINGOLY: A Benchmark of Olympiad-Level Linguistic Reasoning Puzzles in Low-Resource and Extinct Languages (Bean et al., NeurIPS 2024 Datasets & Benchmarks) — defines the Rosetta and Match-Up puzzle formats and documents the low ~21.7% exact-match SOTA headroom this candidate exploits.
  • From Rosetta to Match-Up: A Paired Corpus of Linguistic Puzzles with Human and LLM Benchmarks (Majmudar et al., arXiv 2026) — the namesake; pairs Rosetta-style translation puzzles with Match-Up MC versions, and the Rosetta-to-Match-Up scoring trick is this candidate's load-bearing idea.

Brainstorm parents

  • Invented Tonal/Diacritic Orthography Disambiguation — seeded the diacritic/tone homograph-disambiguation variant with its marker-precedence hierarchy (tone resolves homograph, context particle overrides tone).
  • Candidates-merged c08 entry — origin "both"; merges the tonal/diacritic brainstorm idea with the LINGOLY / Rosetta-to-Match-Up literature line into one MC rule-induction generator.

Dead branches: dropped near-relatives

  • D1 — Inverted Multiple-Choice convention — both sources flag HIGH one-shot risk: a single sentence ("answer the alphabetically-first incorrect option") closes the gap, failing the anti-one-shot criterion. c08 keeps the MC format but earns difficulty from latent-rule distractors rather than a label-inversion trick.
  • D9 / D3 — char-level string-task cluster (StringLLM, CUTE, letter-counting) — dropped as "not a task"; kept only as the red-flag caveat that char-count and free-text-morphology scoring is noisy at temp 0. This caveat is exactly why c08 routes to single-letter MC rather than free-text translation.
  • c07 Case-Marked Micro-Conlang Translation (refuted cousin, now GATED) — the free-text conlang translation sibling was refuted on the optimization path: its gloss table hands the latent morphology to the model in-context and its "hard rule" (case beats word order) is the highest-prior rule in linguistics. c08's MC framing and one-rule-per-distractor construction are the response to that refutation.

Is the surrounding research area still active?

Related active research (2025–2026)
  • Can LLMs Generate and Solve Linguistic Olympiad Puzzles? (Majmudar & Filatova, EMNLP 2025) — same author line as Rosetta-to-Match-Up; establishes LLM generation and solving of Olympiad puzzles, directly supporting the synthetic-generator adaptation c08 requires.
  • Reasoning Over the Glyphs: Evaluation of LLM's Decipherment of Rare Scripts (Shih et al., arXiv 2025) — the "rare-script decipherment (2025)" source; motivates the diacritic/tone homograph-disambiguation variant on unfamiliar writing systems.
  • LingBench++: A Linguistically-Informed Benchmark and Reasoning Framework (Lian et al., arXiv 2025) — a 2025 follow-up in the LingOly line; a multi-step linguistic reasoning benchmark cited as research-heat evidence that the family is still active.

Does the case hold up from every angle?

Perspectives

Round-1 rubric fit

Supports
  • Distractors each differ by exactly one latent rule, giving a built-in incremental ladder with diagnostic wrong answers; MC framing tames scoring into clean 0/1 exact match. Rated a strong research-current dark horse in the round-1 landscape.
Against
  • The exact-match arithmetic/simulation tier simply outscored it at 26/2; a clean design was not enough to reach the 28-tier finalist band.

Optimization-path skeptic

Supports
  • Because each distractor isolates one rule, an optimizer that surfaces one more rule (word order, agreement, affix meaning, diacritic override) measurably eliminates one distractor — an unusually legible gradient for prompt optimization.
Against
  • No dedicated optimization-path skeptic pass was run below the 28-tier finalists, so this lens was never adversarially examined for c08; the inference here is from the design, not a skeptic verdict.

Measurement skeptic

Supports
  • The Rosetta-to-Match-Up conversion is a deliberate answer to the char-level / free-text noise caveat — single-letter output sidesteps the temp-0 morphology-scoring noise that sinks free-text translation.
Against
  • No measurement skeptic pass was run for c08 below the finalist tier; distractor quality (that each really isolates exactly one rule, with no unintended second cue) is asserted by design and flagged for pilot validation, not yet verified.

Implementation lens (round 2)

Supports
  • Oracle independence is strong — a rule-based solver over a seeded grammar is trivially hand-verifiable; redesign debt is low since no structural skeptic refutation is outstanding.
Against
  • The reuse floor is the binding weakness: LINGOLY offers no generator, no seed, no distractor logic, and its dataset is CC-BY-NC-ND (no-derivatives), so I1 caps at 1 and hundreds of lines must be written from scratch borrowing under thirty.

Research currency

Supports
  • The LingOly line is unusually active into 2026 — LingBench++, LingOly-TOO, Rosetta-to-Match-Up, ConlangCrafter — so a synthetic generator here has a natural path to becoming its own research artifact.
Against
  • Every live artifact in the line is either a frozen human-authored set or a translation/generation study; none ships the re-seedable Match-Up generator this candidate needs, so currency does not lower the build cost.

What would it take to build, and what could go wrong?

Implementation: steps & risks

Build the seeded conlang engine

Sample a fresh grammar per run — nonce lexicon plus strata for word order, agreement, affix meaning, and the diacritic/tone precedence hierarchy — with seed plumbing throughout, which is absent entirely in LINGOLY.

Render Rosetta pairs and the Match-Up MC builder

Produce N glossed example pairs plus a held-out query, then construct three distractors where each perturbs exactly one latent rule and one option is the gold pairing.

Write the rule-based oracle and single-letter scorer

Derive the gold answer from the latent grammar (not a pre-stored string), reusing only LINGOLY's clean_answer normalization (~20 LOC) and the safe_exact idea (~5 LOC).

Pilot-validate distractor quality

On a batch, confirm each distractor isolates exactly one rule and no option carries an unintended second cue — the load-bearing engineering per the merged adaptation note.

Risks

  • Rebuild-from-scratch cost: the load-bearing generator, distractor logic, oracle, and seed plumbing do not exist in the repo; estimated hundreds of lines written outside it, borrowing under thirty — the fact that caps I1 at 1.
  • Distractor leakage: if a distractor accidentally violates two rules (or none cleanly), the "one rule eliminates one distractor" ladder degrades and the diagnostic signal is lost — hence the mandated pilot gate.
  • License constraint: LINGOLY's puzzle dataset is CC-BY-NC-ND-4.0 (no-derivatives), so even seeding strata from the human puzzles is legally constrained; the generator must be fully synthetic.
