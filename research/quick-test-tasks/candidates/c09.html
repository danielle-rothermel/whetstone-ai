What does it actually ask the model to do?

What it proposes

Decode a stacked structural cipher back to a fixed closed vocabulary

A pinned generator draws plaintext from a fixed closed word list (e.g. "north gate open"), then encodes it with an ordered stack of two to three named STRUCTURAL reversible operations — keyed block reversal, invented digraph swap, positional skip — deliberately excluding shift arithmetic, which the char-level literature shows is token-noise-prone. Each instance ships the ciphertext, the op stack, and a per-instance key; the model must emit the plaintext words, scored by exact match against the closed vocabulary (or a multiple-choice letter). The latent rules the model must recover are each op's inverse, the apply-inverses-in-reverse-order meta-rule, and the key-derived block sizes. The layer count is the difficulty dial; seeded keys and ops make instances contamination-proof, and because every layer is separable, a failure is diagnostic per layer.

Example instance

Illustrative — constructed from the merged summary, not a corpus fixture. Seeded so the op stack, key, and closed vocabulary are fixed per generator seed; the ciphertext is schematic, not a byte-exact encoder output.

Closed vocabulary: {north, south, gate, wall, open, shut, hold}
Op stack (apply in order): [ block-reverse(k), digraph-swap(TH<->Xz), positional-skip(2) ]
Key: k = 3
Ciphertext:  Xz r o n  ...  (structurally permuted tokens)
Meta-rule to recover: invert each op, applied in REVERSE order
Gold answer: "north gate open"
Naive-prompt miss: a model that inverts the ops in the stated (forward)
order, or that treats digraph-swap as a Caesar shift, decodes to a
non-vocabulary string and scores 0 under exact match.

Where does the design come from?

Academic anchor & lineage

Literature parents

  • CipherBank (Li et al., ACL Findings 2025) — the core anchor: a 2,358-problem cipher-decryption benchmark with exact-match scoring, showing char-level ciphers are noisy and hard even for reasoning models; the source of the salvageable single-layer encoders.
  • Jailbreaking LLMs via Novel Complex Ciphers / ACE+LACE (Handa et al., arXiv 2024) — the layered custom-encryption (LACE) methodology; the true referent of the corpus's ambiguous "CipherBench novel-cipher methodology" tag (see dead branches).
  • ICL CIPHERS (Fang et al., arXiv 2025) — substitution-cipher reformulation to quantify genuine in-context learning, contamination-proof by construction.
  • StringLLM (Wang et al., ICLR 2025) — design-constraint caveat: LLMs are poor at string processing, motivating the closed-vocab / MC framing over raw character decode.
  • CUTE (Edman et al., EMNLP 2024) — design-constraint caveat: LLMs mishandle their own tokens and spelling, justifying the avoidance of char-arithmetic to protect temp-0 resolvability.

Brainstorm parents

  • Composable Cipher Chain Resolution (claude-opus-4-8[1m]) — proposed the ordered stack of named reversible layers; the structural backbone of c09.
  • Cipher-With-Invented-Rules Decoder (first-principles) — a near-duplicate per-position-shift decoder, merged in as the invented-rules framing.
  • c09 · Composable Cipher-Chain Decode (merged entry) — combined the two brainstorm items with the CipherBank literature into the closed-vocab design.

Dead branches: dropped near-relatives

  • D9 · Char-level string-task cluster (StringLLM, CUTE, letter-counting, Divide-and-Conquer) — not a task; kept only as the red-flag design caveat that justifies c09's closed-vocab / MC framing. Char-level shift decode was pulled out of scope precisely because it is token-noise-prone at temp 0.
  • D3 · TokenCounter (distinct vowel/consonant counting) — letter counting is tokenization-bound; the StringLLM/CUTE literature shows char-count tasks are noisy at temp 0, threatening temp-0 resolvability — the same reason c09 dropped shift arithmetic.
  • "CipherBench v2" (cipherbench.github.io) — a provenance-ambiguous, pseudonymous github-pages benchmark with no paper and a different scope; treated as a laundered citation. The methodology the corpus meant is Handa et al. 2024, listed above.

Is the surrounding research area still active?

Related active research (2025–2026)
  • Rashid (Bafna et al., arXiv 2026) — a cipher-based framework generating unseen languages for in-context language learning; the corpus paired it with ICL Ciphers under the label "Rashid" (which is the paper title, not an author surname).
  • HERO'S JOURNEY: Testing Complex Rule Induction with Text Games (arXiv 2026) — a complex multi-rule induction benchmark directly adjacent to the composed-op cipher-chain difficulty dial; named elsewhere in the corpus but not on c09's own source list, and the URL was not fetched.
  • MIR-Bench (Yan et al., 2025) — many-shot in-context function/string-transformation induction, relevant to the demo axis and layered-rule inference; URL inferred, not fetched.

What would it take to build, and what could go wrong?

Implementation: steps & risks

Author a seeded chain generator

Import the five structural (non-shift) cipher classes from CipherBank's cipher/encryption.py unchanged (Atbash, Reverse, SwapPairs, Polybius, DualAvgCode), add a per-instance random.Random(seed), sample a chain of K transforms, and fold them over the plaintext — the composition and re-seeding the repo lacks (~120–180 new LOC outside the repo).

Build the closed-vocabulary / nonce source

Replace the frozen 261-sentence data/plaintext.jsonl with a seeded emitter that draws from a fixed closed word list, so the gold answer is one of a known set and an MC option bank can be generated (~40–60 LOC).

Wire the oracle and runner

Reuse compare_strings (utils/tools.py:261) verbatim for the 0/1 exact match, drop Levenshtein, add MC-option matching, and supply our own single-LLM-call prompt — discarding the broken test.py, run.sh, model clients, and the import-time global in prompt.py.

Calibrate the difficulty dial

Pin the layer-count strata so the full-rule reference prompt reaches near-ceiling, balance the closed-vocab / MC answer distribution against a constant-guess baseline, and confirm the reverse-order meta-rule is per-layer diagnostic.

Risks

  • Codebase gives primitives, not a generator: CipherBank is a frozen 261-item dataset with no chaining and no seeding; CryptoX hardcodes random.seed(42) inside its sampler and implements only single-layer per-character substitution — so re-seedability and composition are surgery we own, not config.
  • Temp-0 resolvability still rides on the op choice: the whole design exists to dodge char-arithmetic noise; if any structural op degrades to character shuffling under BPE, the temp-0 stability the closed-vocab framing was meant to protect can slip, and this has never been empirically gated for c09.
  • Provenance gaps: CipherBank ships no LICENSE file despite an MIT badge (paper-assumable but unconfirmed); CryptoX has no license and Chinese-only docstrings; one corpus methodology citation ("CipherBench v2") is a laundered, unverifiable source.
