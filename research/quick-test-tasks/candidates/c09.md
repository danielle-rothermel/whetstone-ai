## What it proposes

### Decode a stacked structural cipher back to a fixed closed vocabulary

A pinned generator draws plaintext from a fixed closed word list (e.g. "north gate open"), then encodes it with an **ordered stack of two to three named STRUCTURAL reversible operations** — keyed block reversal, invented digraph swap, positional skip — deliberately excluding shift arithmetic, which the char-level literature shows is token-noise-prone. Each instance ships the **ciphertext, the op stack, and a per-instance key**; the model must emit the plaintext words, scored by exact match against the **closed vocabulary** (or a multiple-choice letter). The latent rules the model must recover are each op's inverse, the apply-inverses-in-**reverse-order** meta-rule, and the key-derived block sizes. The **layer count is the difficulty dial**; seeded keys and ops make instances contamination-proof, and because every layer is separable, a failure is diagnostic per layer.

### Example instance (illustrative — constructed from the merged summary, not a corpus fixture)

Seeded so the op stack, key, and closed vocabulary are fixed per generator seed; the ciphertext below is schematic, not a byte-exact encoder output.

```
Closed vocabulary: {north, south, gate, wall, open, shut, hold}
Op stack (apply in order): [ block-reverse(k), digraph-swap(TH<->Xz), positional-skip(2) ]
Key: k = 3
Ciphertext:  Xz r o n  ...  (structurally permuted tokens)
Meta-rule to recover: invert each op, applied in REVERSE order
Gold answer: "north gate open"
Naive-prompt miss: a model that inverts the ops in the stated (forward)
order, or that treats digraph-swap as a Caesar shift, decodes to a
non-vocabulary string and scores 0 under exact match.
```

## Academic anchor & lineage

### Literature parents

- [CipherBank (Li et al., ACL Findings 2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.19093) — the core anchor: a 2,358-problem cipher-decryption benchmark with exact-match scoring, demonstrating that char-level ciphers are noisy and hard even for reasoning models; the source of the salvageable single-layer encoders.
- [Jailbreaking LLMs via Novel Complex Ciphers / ACE+LACE (Handa et al., arXiv 2024)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.10601) — the layered custom-encryption (LACE) methodology; the true referent of the corpus's ambiguous "CipherBench novel-cipher methodology" tag (see dead branches).
- [ICL CIPHERS (Fang et al., arXiv 2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.19395) — substitution-cipher reformulation to quantify genuine in-context learning, contamination-proof by construction.
- [StringLLM (Wang et al., arXiv 2024 / ICLR 2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.01208) — design-constraint caveat: LLMs are poor at string processing, motivating the closed-vocab / MC framing over raw character decode.
- [CUTE (Edman et al., EMNLP 2024)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.15452) — design-constraint caveat: LLMs mishandle their own tokens and spelling, justifying the avoidance of char-arithmetic to protect temp-0 resolvability.

### Brainstorm parents

- **Composable Cipher Chain Resolution** (`claude-opus-4-8[1m]`) — proposed the ordered stack of named reversible layers; the structural backbone of c09.
- **Cipher-With-Invented-Rules Decoder** (first-principles) — a near-duplicate per-position-shift decoder, merged in as the invented-rules framing.
- Merged entry [c09 · Composable Cipher-Chain Decode (candidates-merged.md)](../candidates-merged.md#c09) — combined the two brainstorm items with the CipherBank literature into the closed-vocab design.

### Dead branches: dropped near-relatives

- **D9 · Char-level string-task cluster** (StringLLM, CUTE, letter-counting, Divide-and-Conquer) — not a task; kept only as the red-flag design caveat that *justifies* c09's closed-vocab / MC framing. Char-level shift decode was pulled out of scope precisely because it is token-noise-prone at temp 0.
- **D3 · TokenCounter (distinct vowel/consonant counting)** — letter counting is tokenization-bound; the StringLLM/CUTE literature shows char-count tasks are noisy at temp 0, directly threatening the temp-0 resolvability criterion — the same reason c09 dropped shift arithmetic.
- **"CipherBench v2" (cipherbench.github.io)** — a provenance-ambiguous, pseudonymous github-pages benchmark with no paper and a different scope; treated as a laundered citation. The methodology the corpus meant is Handa et al. 2024, already listed above.

## Perspectives

### Round-1 rubric fit
- **Supports:** Scored a solid 26 with the full tie-breaker for research currency; the closed-vocab / MC answer surface, per-instance key, and layer-count dial map cleanly onto contamination-resistance and bounded output.
- **Against:** It sat in the 26 tier, below the 27/28 finalists, and never earned a dedicated adversarial write-up in round 1 — its rubric note is a single design line, not a defended scorecard.

### Optimization-path skeptic
- **Supports:** The apply-inverses-in-reverse-order meta-rule and key-derived block sizes are arbitrary seeded information no zero-shot prompt can contain, so the incremental ladder is structural, not tuned.
- **Against:** No skeptic lens actually examined c09 in round 1 — the adversarial optimization-path passes were run only on the 28-tier finalists plus c03, so this lens is inferred, not verified for this candidate.

### Measurement skeptic
- **Supports:** The oracle is the human-authored source plaintext carried through untouched, independent of both the encode and decode paths, and the exact-match scorer maps directly onto a clean 0/1 signal.
- **Against:** Likewise never subjected to a round-1 measurement refutation pass; the temp-0 resolvability concern that forced the structural-op redesign is a design assumption that has not been empirically gated on this candidate.

### Implementation lens (round 2)
- **Supports:** Two independent repo reads confirm reusable, well-written deterministic encoders with a genuinely independent exact-match oracle, ~320 LOC of salvageable primitives, and only verification-level redesign debt.
- **Against:** Both CipherBank and CryptoX are frozen datasets with a hardcoded seed, no cipher composition, and no closed-vocab / MC / nonce support — the seeding, chaining, vocabulary, and MC layers are all net-new, so the diff is a partial rebuild, capping the double-weighted codebase cell at 1 and the total at 9/16.

### Research currency
- **Supports:** Cipher-decode and in-context substitution-cipher work is actively published through 2025–2026 (CipherBank ACL 2025, ICL Ciphers, Rashid 2026), a contamination-proof line with genuine standalone-benchmark potential.
- **Against:** There is no flagship live leaderboard to anchor to, and the "CipherBench" methodology tag the corpus leaned on turned out to be an unverifiable pseudonymous site, weakening one of its provenance claims.

## Implementation: steps & risks

1. **Author a seeded chain generator.** Import the five structural (non-shift) cipher classes from CipherBank's `cipher/encryption.py` unchanged (Atbash, Reverse, SwapPairs, Polybius, DualAvgCode), add a per-instance `random.Random(seed)`, sample a chain of K transforms, and fold them over the plaintext — the composition and re-seeding the repo lacks (~120–180 new LOC outside the repo).
2. **Build the closed-vocabulary / nonce source.** Replace the frozen 261-sentence `data/plaintext.jsonl` with a seeded emitter that draws from a fixed closed word list, so the gold answer is one of a known set and an MC option bank can be generated (~40–60 LOC).
3. **Wire the oracle and runner.** Reuse `compare_strings` (`utils/tools.py:261`) verbatim for the 0/1 exact match, drop Levenshtein, add MC-option matching, and supply our own single-LLM-call prompt — discarding the broken `test.py`, `run.sh`, model clients, and the import-time global in `prompt.py`.
4. **Calibrate the difficulty dial.** Pin the layer-count strata so the full-rule reference prompt reaches near-ceiling, balance the closed-vocab / MC answer distribution against a constant-guess baseline, and confirm the reverse-order meta-rule is per-layer diagnostic.

### Risks
- **Codebase gives primitives, not a generator.** CipherBank is a frozen 261-item dataset with no chaining and no seeding; CryptoX hardcodes `random.seed(42)` inside its sampler and implements only single-layer per-character substitution — so re-seedability and composition are surgery we own, not config.
- **Temp-0 resolvability still rides on the op choice.** The whole design exists to dodge char-arithmetic noise; if any structural op degrades to character shuffling under BPE, the temp-0 stability the closed-vocab framing was meant to protect can slip, and this has never been empirically gated for c09.
- **Provenance gaps.** CipherBank ships no LICENSE file despite an MIT badge (paper-assumable but unconfirmed); CryptoX has no license and Chinese-only docstrings; one corpus methodology citation ("CipherBench v2") is a laundered, unverifiable source.

### Repo review & run verification
- **Codebase:** [Leey21/CipherBank](https://github.com/Leey21/CipherBank) (no LICENSE file; MIT badge, paper-assumable) — deep-read notes at [repos/cipherbank.md](../repos/cipherbank.md).
- **Second codebase:** [multimodal-art-projection/CryptoX](https://github.com/multimodal-art-projection/CryptoX) (no license, arXiv 2502.07813 attached) — deep-read notes at [repos/cryptox-cryptobench.md](../repos/cryptox-cryptobench.md).
