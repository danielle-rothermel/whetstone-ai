## What it proposes

### Convert a quantity through an invented, per-instance unit ladder to a canonical form

A seeded generator invents a **unit ladder** with mixed radices — no real-world ratios — and prints an inline **conversion legend** ("1 grol = 7 blens, 1 blen = 12 mirs"), then poses one conversion request. The model returns either a single integer in the smallest unit or a **canonical mixed-radix string** ("4 grol 0 blen 2 mir"), scored by exact match against a trivial **mixed-radix reducer**. Difficulty decomposes into independent latent rules: legend parse, per-boundary factors, carry and normalization so no unit exceeds its base, a truncation-versus-rounding keyword, one signed-unit stratum, and unit-simplification rewrites. Because ratios and names are randomized per instance, the prompt must encode the general procedure rather than memorized numbers.

### Example instance

*Illustrative, constructed faithfully from the merged summary — invented three-unit ladder, mixed radices 7 and 12, carry-normalized answer.*

```
Legend: 1 grol = 7 blens, 1 blen = 12 mirs
Query:  Express 350 mirs in canonical grol/blen/mir form.

Reduce: 350 mirs = 29 blens + 2 mirs
        29 blens  =  4 grol  + 1 blen
Gold:   4 grol 1 blen 2 mir

Naive-prompt miss: model treats both boundaries as base 10
(or divides by 84 without carrying the blen remainder),
returning "4 grol 0 blen 2 mir" or a bare "350".
```

## Academic anchor & lineage

### Literature parents

- [NUMCoT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.02864) (Xu et al., arXiv 2024) — the core anchor; documents that LLMs consistently fail to apply numeral and units-of-measurement conversion ratios in chain-of-thought, and mixed-radix systems (time, imperial, ancient units) are its native ground. Source of the low-floor claim.
- [GSM-Symbolic](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.05229) (Mirzadeh et al., ICLR 2025) — donates the per-instance symbolic-template methodology with exact ground truth; c02 borrows the templating and perturbation idea, not the natural-language narrative.
- [GSM-Plus](https://aclanthology.org/2024.acl-long.163/) (Li et al., ACL 2024) — robustness benchmark adding numeric and distractor perturbations to GSM8K; c02 reuses the perturbation-templating idea only.
- [GSM8K](https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.14168) (Cobbe et al., arXiv 2021) — the lineage root underlying both GSM-Symbolic and GSM-Plus, hence the template-generation methodology c02 inherits at one remove.

*Attribution correction:* NUMCoT is cited elsewhere in the corpus as "Wang et al."; the canonical arXiv:2406.02864 first author is Ancheng Xu, so "Xu et al., 2024" is used here.

## Perspectives

### Round-1 rubric fit

- **Supports:** Trivial reference reducer plus single-integer or canonical-string output gives exact deterministic scoring with a known near-100% ceiling when the legend is fully stated. Per-instance randomized ratios and names buy contamination resistance and force the prompt to encode a general procedure.
- **Against:** Scored one rung below the finalists, landing at the rubric floor of the invented-notation cluster. The published task is natural-language and CoT-heavy, so the narrative must be stripped and output hard-bounded before the fit holds.

### Optimization-path skeptic

- **Supports:** Stacking rounding, signed-unit, and simplification strata means single-rule prompts leave points on the table, keeping the gap multi-step rather than one-shot.
- **Against:** No dedicated skeptic pass was run on c02 — it sits below the tier that received optimization-path and measurement verdicts, so this lens never formally examined it.

### Measurement skeptic

- **Supports:** A canonical carry-normalized form and 0/1 exact match remove partial-credit ambiguity and resolve cleanly at temperature zero.
- **Against:** No measurement verdict was recorded below the finalist tier; the landscape note flags only overlap with c01/c10 and carries no repair or redesign flag, so mandated-fix status is unexamined.

### Implementation lens (round 2)

- **Supports:** Now official ranking evidence — the ground-truth math is a trivial positional weighted sum that an independent second path confirms, and add-a-rule or fix-a-bug touches one arithmetic function with no cross-rule coupling.
- **Against:** Six-plus latent rules including signed-digit and simplification lift the moving-part count above a minimal design; the scores were folded into the official ranking by the 2026-07-21 amendment, scored via the same pipeline post-hoc, and were never run-verified.

### Research currency

- **Supports:** Unit-conversion brittleness and inductive rule-learning are actively studied (NUMCoT, GSM-Symbolic ICLR 2025 and its re-evaluations, InductionBench ACL 2025).
- **Against:** It was initially excluded on research currency by the round-2 tie-breaker before the 2026-07-21 amendment folded it in; it still carries the lower currency value while overlapping cousins c01 and c10 cover the same invented-notation ground and outrank it.

## Implementation: steps & risks

1. **Build a seeded invented-ladder generator.** Add a re-seedable per-instance RNG and sample an invented unit ladder with mixed radices into a per-run conversion table, sample a quantity, and render the legend plus one-line question — this is entirely new plumbing since NUMCoT has no seeding anywhere and its radices are hard-coded literals.
2. **Write the exact reducer oracle around a passed-in table.** Reduce to the base unit and re-express canonically over the sampled table, verified by an independent second path (multiply place-weights and sum, versus carry-normalization); discard NUMCoT's buggy solve_hard oracle and its double-subtraction correction.
3. **Stratify the latent rules and glue single-call scoring.** Add ladder depth, radix magnitude, the truncation-versus-rounding keyword, the signed-unit stratum, and unit-simplification rewrites as knobs, then wire one LLM call plus 0/1 exact-match on canonical form, dropping the repo's substring-regex scorer, five-prompt fan-out, threads, and Windows CSV I/O.
4. **Calibrate the floor and add tests.** Tune the default prompt to land mid-range and calibrate the rounding and signed-unit strata so the score climbs rung-by-rung, adding determinism-by-seed, oracle round-trip, and hand-checked boundary tests that the repo entirely lacks.

### Risks

- The NUMCoT code is a design reference only, not config-plus-glue — serving c02 is a rewrite of generator, oracle, and scoring, so the effort is major surgery on effectively frozen code (impl I1 = 1).
- Six-plus latent rules including a signed-digit stratum and simplification rewrites raise the moving-part count and the number of pre-freeze calibration gates, risking a floor that does not climb cleanly rung-by-rung.
- Overlap with c01 and c10 means the invented-notation ground is already covered; without a distinguishing angle the task adds little beyond the finalists.
