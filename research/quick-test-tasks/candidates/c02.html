What does the task ask the model to do?

What it proposes

Convert a quantity through an invented, per-instance unit ladder to a canonical form

A seeded generator invents a unit ladder with mixed radices — no real-world ratios — and prints an inline conversion legend ("1 grol = 7 blens, 1 blen = 12 mirs"), then poses one conversion request. The model returns either a single integer in the smallest unit or a canonical mixed-radix string ("4 grol 0 blen 2 mir"), scored by exact match against a trivial mixed-radix reducer. Difficulty decomposes into independent latent rules: legend parse, per-boundary factors, carry and normalization so no unit exceeds its base, a truncation-versus-rounding keyword, one signed-unit stratum, and unit-simplification rewrites. Because ratios and names are randomized per instance, the prompt must encode the general procedure rather than memorized numbers.

Example instance

Illustrative, constructed faithfully from the merged summary — invented three-unit ladder, mixed radices 7 and 12, carry-normalized answer.

Legend: 1 grol = 7 blens, 1 blen = 12 mirs
Query:  Express 350 mirs in canonical grol/blen/mir form.

Reduce: 350 mirs = 29 blens + 2 mirs
        29 blens  =  4 grol  + 1 blen
Gold:   4 grol 1 blen 2 mir

Naive-prompt miss: model treats both boundaries as base 10
(or divides by 84 without carrying the blen remainder),
returning "4 grol 0 blen 2 mir" or a bare "350".

Where does this task come from?

Academic anchor & lineage

Literature parents

  • NUMCoT (Xu et al., arXiv 2024) — the core anchor; documents that LLMs consistently fail to apply numeral and units-of-measurement conversion ratios in chain-of-thought, and mixed-radix systems (time, imperial, ancient units) are its native ground. Source of the low-floor claim.
  • GSM-Symbolic (Mirzadeh et al., ICLR 2025) — donates the per-instance symbolic-template methodology with exact ground truth; c02 borrows the templating and perturbation idea, not the natural-language narrative.
  • GSM-Plus (Li et al., ACL 2024) — robustness benchmark adding numeric and distractor perturbations to GSM8K; c02 reuses the perturbation-templating idea only.
  • GSM8K (Cobbe et al., arXiv 2021) — the lineage root underlying both GSM-Symbolic and GSM-Plus, hence the template-generation methodology c02 inherits at one remove.

Attribution correction: NUMCoT is cited elsewhere in the corpus as "Wang et al."; the canonical arXiv:2406.02864 first author is Ancheng Xu, so "Xu et al., 2024" is used here.

Brainstorm parents

  • Notation Unit-System Conversion — the seed idea of a legend-plus-quantity conversion problem over a bespoke unit system.
  • Symbolic unit / dimension algebra — contributed the unit-simplification-rewrite stratum and the notion of algebra over invented dimensions.
  • Unit-Convention Numeric QA — supplied the single-question, single-answer framing that keeps output bounded and exactly scorable.

Dead branches: dropped near-relatives

  • Interval-Overlap Field Selection (dropped cluster D2) — only two or three boundary/tie conventions, enumerable in one prompt, so the boundary flavor was absorbed into c12/c13 strata rather than kept as a task.
  • Contaminated real-unit conversion (imperial/metric direct) — real-world ratios are contaminated and one-shot memorizable; the adaptation mandates inventing all units, which is why NUMCoT ships only as a design reference, not a reusable dataset.
  • NumericBench-style numeracy breadth — folded into c01's source list as reference-only literature; most sub-tasks are off-scope and standard-notation arithmetic is near-ceiling without a notation swap.

What live 2025-2026 research backs it?

  • InductionBench (Hua et al., arXiv 2025) — evaluates inductive rule-inference from examples; directly relevant to c02's withheld-ratio strata, where the conversion procedure must be induced from demos rather than stated.
  • GSM-Symbolic (Mirzadeh et al., ICLR 2025) — the active-research face of the templating methodology c02 borrows; its published fragility curve is the closest evidence that per-instance perturbation moves accuracy incrementally.
  • A Critical Re-evaluation of GSM-Symbolic (Dlugosz et al., arXiv 2026) — statistical re-evaluation of GSM-Symbolic's conclusions, bearing on the methodology c02 borrows; single-fetch only and the arXiv id (2605.28700) is anomalous, so provenance is treated as provisional.

What does it take to build, and what can go wrong?

Implementation: steps & risks

Build a seeded invented-ladder generator

Add a re-seedable per-instance RNG and sample an invented unit ladder with mixed radices into a per-run conversion table, sample a quantity, and render the legend plus one-line question — this is entirely new plumbing since NUMCoT has no seeding anywhere and its radices are hard-coded literals

Write the exact reducer oracle around a passed-in table

Reduce to the base unit and re-express canonically over the sampled table, verified by an independent second path (multiply place-weights and sum, versus carry-normalization); discard NUMCoT's buggy solve_hard oracle and its double-subtraction correction

Stratify the latent rules and glue single-call scoring

Add ladder depth, radix magnitude, the truncation-versus-rounding keyword, the signed-unit stratum, and unit-simplification rewrites as knobs, then wire one LLM call plus 0/1 exact-match on canonical form, dropping the repo's substring-regex scorer, five-prompt fan-out, threads, and Windows CSV I/O

Calibrate the floor and add tests

Tune the default prompt to land mid-range and calibrate the rounding and signed-unit strata so the score climbs rung-by-rung, adding determinism-by-seed, oracle round-trip, and hand-checked boundary tests that the repo entirely lacks

Risks

  • The NUMCoT code is a design reference only, not config-plus-glue — serving c02 is a rewrite of generator, oracle, and scoring, so the effort is major surgery on effectively frozen code (impl I1 = 1)
  • Six-plus latent rules including a signed-digit stratum and simplification rewrites raise the moving-part count and the number of pre-freeze calibration gates, risking a floor that does not climb cleanly rung-by-rung
  • Overlap with c01 and c10 means the invented-notation ground is already covered; without a distinguishing angle the task adds little beyond the finalists

Repo review & run verification

  • Codebase: CAS-SIAT-XinHai/NUMCoT (CC0-1.0) — deep-read notes at repos/numcot.md. The provided TIGER-AI-Lab lead 404s; this is the real ACL 2024 repo.
  • Run verification: not run never run-verified; the shipped experiment consumes frozen CSVs, the generator/oracle is uncalled dead code with no seeding, and solve_hard is provably buggy, so nothing was seed-checked or oracle-agreement-checked.
