# c19 · Grid-World State Prediction — ADOPT

**You know this as:** grid-world state tracking — the bAbI/BabyAI "predict the final state after a move sequence" task, reframed to one derived fact.

Status: **ADOPT · ranked #1 of 20 in round 2.**
Domain: deterministic spatial micro-simulation.

## Verdict

**Adopt — ranked first after the implementation-aware rescore.** c19 wins on the single fact that
dominated round 2: it is the only candidate that pairs a genuinely reusable, **run-verified**
codebase with skeptic debt that is verification-only rather than structural. Farama-Foundation's
Minigrid is Apache-2.0, actively maintained, and — confirmed by a fresh run — properly seed-threaded,
so the same seed reproduces byte-identical grids and different seeds vary. The entire adaptation is
roughly a hundred-and-a-half lines of configuration, an independent derived-fact oracle, and strict
0/1 scoring glue that lives **outside** the repo; nothing inside `minigrid/` is edited. Round 1
scored the design in the strong middle of the pool and flagged only one soft spot — grid navigation
is a familiar enough skill that a naive prompt might not floor low — which the invented-command and
wrap-versus-clamp convention strata are designed to counter. Because those nonstandard dynamics are
net-new logic we write on top of Minigrid rather than something the repo supplies, the build is real
but bounded, and the standing reflects that: a mid-pack round-1 task that rose two tiers to the top on
implementation strength.

## What it proposes

### The task: predict one derived fact from a small seeded grid, exact-match

A seeded generator builds a small ASCII grid (roughly six-by-six) with **walls**, a **robot pose**,
and a short **command string** that mixes ordinary moves with two or three **invented commands** (for
example `J` = jump-two, `W` = wrap). The model predicts exactly **one derived fact** — a final
**coordinate**, a **heading**, or a **carrying-flag** — never a path, which removes the many-valid-
answers ambiguity that plagues plan-generation grid tasks. Output is a single token (`3,5` or `E`),
scored by exact match against a tiny independent simulator. The **latent rules** that vary across
strata are relative-turn semantics, wall-blocking versus edge wrap or clamp, the invented-command
semantics (including jump-through-wall behavior), and the origin/indexing convention — varied so that
no single fixed convention statement can cover the whole pool. Randomized grids are contamination-
proof, and an off-by-one-at-a-wall failure is cleanly diagnostic of which rule the prompt missed.

### Example instance (illustrative)

Seeded conditions: a five-by-five grid, robot at `(1,1)` facing east, edge behavior = clamp,
one wall column, invented `J` = jump-two-in-facing-direction (passes through walls).

```
Grid (row,col; origin top-left):
. . # . .
. R . . .
. . # . .
. . # . .
. . . . .

Rules given by prompt: L/R = turn 90°, F = step forward, J = jump 2 forward (ignores walls),
edges clamp (no wrap).
Command: F F J R F

Query: final coordinate of R?
Gold: 3,3

Naive-prompt miss: a model that treats J as a single step, or that lets the wall
at (2,2)/(3,2) block the jump, lands at 2,3 or 1,3 — the wrong-rule signature is legible.
```

## Academic anchor & lineage

### Literature parents

- **GridRoute (Li et al., arXiv 2025)** — https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.24306 — cardinal-movement grid
  route-planning benchmark; c19 flips its plan-generation into single-token state prediction.
- **GRASP (Tang & Kejriwal, arXiv 2024)** — https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.01892 — sixteen-thousand
  grid-based commonsense spatial-reasoning environments; the direct grid-world antecedent.
- **LLM-BabyBench (Choukrani et al., arXiv 2025)** — https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.12135 — textual BabyAI
  grid world; its Predict split (final state after an action sequence) is the c19 template.
- **bAbI tasks (Weston et al., arXiv 2015)** — https://arxiv.org/abs/1502.05698 — classic synthetic
  entity/world-state tracking QA; c19 regenerates its state-tracking flavor over grids.
- **Recurrent Entity Networks / EntNet (Henaff et al., ICLR 2017)** — https://arxiv.org/abs/1612.03969
  — world-state tracking model that solves bAbI; cited as evidence for state-tracking difficulty and
  ceiling.
- **Entity Tracking in Language Models (Kim & Schuster, ACL 2023)** — https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.02363
  — probes LM tracking of an entity's final state after operations; motivates the single-derived-fact
  framing.

### Brainstorm parents

- Grid Robot Deterministic Navigator (brainstorm) — the seed idea: a deterministic robot walking a
  small grid under stated movement rules.
- [c19 in candidates-merged.md](../candidates-merged.md#c19) — origin "both"; the merge fused the
  brainstorm navigator with the four literature grid/state-tracking lines above into a single
  state-prediction task.

### Dead branches: dropped near-relatives

- **D5 · Grounded grid compositional (gSCAN / ReaSCAN)** — dropped: grid rendering plus
  action-sequence outputs are the most expensive things in the pool to bound, and the reviewer judged
  it "not worth the adaptation cost." Grid coverage was assigned to c19, compositional splits to c23.
- **D4 · CRUXEval-style code-output prediction** — dropped: the published set is heavily contaminated
  and regeneration needs a code DSL with unconstrained return types that fight exact-match bounding;
  execution flavor went to c05, rule-induction flavor to c23. (A cousin execution-simulation branch,
  not a grid task, but killed on the same bounding grounds.)

## Perspectives

### Round-1 rubric fit
- **Supports:** asking for one derived fact rather than a path removes the many-valid-answers ambiguity
  and yields a bounded single-token exact-match target; randomized grids are contamination-proof and
  off-by-one-at-a-wall failures are cleanly diagnostic.
- **Against:** grid navigation is a familiar skill, so the naive-prompt floor risks sitting too high
  unless the invented-command and wrap-versus-clamp strata push it down — the one soft spot round 1
  named.

### Optimization-path skeptic
- **Supports:** the latent rules (turn semantics, wrap versus clamp, invented-command behavior,
  indexing) are varied so no single fixed convention statement covers the pool, keeping decisive
  knowledge outside score-only instruction rephrasing.
- **Against:** no dedicated round-1 skeptic pass examined c19 below the 28-tier — round-1
  recommendations.md analyzes only the five finalists (c21, c03, c14, c11, c12), so the optimization-
  path lens on c19 is inferred from the merged summary, not an adversarial verdict.

### Measurement skeptic
- **Supports:** ground truth is read directly from the constructed grid via an independent object-model
  walk, not re-derived from the sampler, and three of three run-verified hand-checks matched — the
  oracle is independent and human-verifiable in under a minute.
- **Against:** likewise never given a formal measurement-skeptic pass in round 1; the ceiling and
  per-stratum floors remain unmeasured pre-freeze gates rather than confirmed properties.

### Implementation lens (round 2)
- **Supports:** the only run-verified I1=3 codebase in the pool with verification-only skeptic debt —
  properly threaded seeding, byte-identical reproduction, roughly a hundred-and-a-half lines of glue,
  and zero edits inside `minigrid/`.
- **Against:** Minigrid supplies generation, serialization, and determinism but **not** the nonstandard
  dynamics — the invented-command and wrap-versus-clamp convention layer is net-new simulation logic we
  write, which is where the real build effort sits (I3=1).

### Research currency
- **Supports:** grid-world state prediction is live in 2025-2026 — MazeEval and the 2026 spatial-world-
  models maze study both probe exactly this floor and its format sensitivity, and Minigrid is actively
  used in current work.
- **Against:** grid navigation is a heavily trodden benchmark family, so the contribution is a careful
  reframing rather than a new problem, and spatial reasoning carries known modality confounds that must
  be controlled by keeping grids small and ASCII.

## Implementation: steps & risks

1. **Day 1-2 — build the harness.** Vendor nothing; add Minigrid as a dependency. Write the
   roughly-hundred-and-a-half-line glue package: a seeded instance generator over stochastic-layout
   envs (Fetch, Crossing, FourRooms, Empty-Random), text render via `pprint_grid`, the invented-command
   and convention layer (wrap versus clamp, indexing, two or three invented commands) as a tiny stepper
   over `grid.encode` state, an independent derived-fact oracle, and a strict 0/1 scorer.
2. **Day 2-3 — freeze gates.** Run a reference "state-all-rules" ceiling prompt at temperature zero;
   measure per-stratum floors; run a red-team check that no single fixed convention statement covers the
   pool. Pin seeds and a manifest.
3. **Wire strata and scoring.** Vary env id and grid size as strata (seed = instance index), optionally
   remap `COLOR_NAMES` / `IDX_TO_OBJECT` to nonce tokens as pure text post-processing, then a single LLM
   call compared against the oracle for exact match.

### Risks

- The invented-command and wrap-versus-clamp convention strata that defeat one-shot guessing are
  **our** net-new simulation-layer logic; Minigrid does not supply nonstandard dynamics, so a bug there
  is a bug in the part that makes the task hard.
- Fixed-layout envs do not vary by seed (`Empty-8x8` is identical for seeds 7 and 8) — strata must use
  the stochastic-layout envs, and the off-path unseeded spots (`wrappers.py:800`, the WFC subpackage)
  must be avoided.
- Roughly two to three pre-freeze gates remain (measured ceiling, per-stratum floors, single-fixed-
  convention red-team) — real calibration, not zero.
- Minor license-metadata mismatch: the LICENSE file says Apache-2.0 while `pyproject` says MIT; cite the
  LICENSE file.

### Repo review & run verification

- **Codebase:** [Farama-Foundation/Minigrid](https://github.com/Farama-Foundation/Minigrid)
  (Apache-2.0, v3.1.0) — deep-read notes at [repos/farama-foundation-minigrid.md](../repos/farama-foundation-minigrid.md).
- **Run verification: PASS.** Seed 42 is byte-identical across runs on five envs; seed 42 versus 43
  differs; three of three ground-truth spot checks matched an independent object-model walk. Seeding is
  threaded through `reset(seed)` → `self.np_random` with no module-global seeding.
