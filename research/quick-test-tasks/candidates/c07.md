## What it proposes

### Task: learn a per-run agglutinative micro-grammar in context, then translate one phrase to a rigid template

A generator builds a fresh agglutinative micro-language on every run: random roots plus affixes that mark case, number, and tense, together with a topic-fronting particle. One instance is a single conlang sentence accompanied by a tiny gloss table; the model must return an English paraphrase in a rigid three-to-six-word template with fixed articles and fixed slot order, scored by exact match after trivial normalization. The latent rules are meant to layer: a **case suffix** fixes subject/object independent of position (the load-bearing rule naive SVO prompts miss), **number** and **tense** affixes, article insertion, a fronting reorder, and an inflection stratum with allomorphy. Ground truth is a deterministic template filler; randomized morphology per run is contamination-proof, and MTOB/LingOly document large headroom for grammar-from-context learning.

### Example instance

*Illustrative — reconstructed faithfully from the merged summary; the corpus gives no canonical instance for this gated candidate.*

```
Gloss table:
  roots:   tavu = dog,  miron = cat,  kela = chase
  affixes: -ok = subject (nominative),  -ip = object (accusative)
           -sa = plural,  za = (topic-fronting particle, sentence-initial)

Conlang sentence:   za mironip tavuok kela
Rigid EN template:  [art] [subj] [verb] [art] [obj]
Gold answer:        the dog chases the cat

Naive-SVO miss:     "the cat chases the dog"
  — a word-order prompt reads the fronted "mironip" (topic particle za + object
    suffix -ip) as the subject; the case suffix, given right in the gloss, is
    what the model was supposed to apply.
```

The miss shows the whole problem: the rule that produces the error is spelled out in the gloss table the model is handed, so recovering it is comprehension, not induction.

## Perspectives

### Round-1 rubric fit
- **Supports:** On paper the best all-round fit in Theme B — cheap, exact-scored, generatable in the hundreds, contamination-proof, latent-rule-decomposed, and few-shot-friendly; scores 27 on structure with a full tie-breaker.
- **Against:** The 27 is structural only; the load-bearing rules are either handed to the model in the per-instance gloss or predicted by universal linguistic priors, so the incremental clause of criterion 4 fails and a single competent paragraph one-shots much of the gap.

### Optimization-path skeptic
- **Supports:** The few-shot glossed pairs are a natural, honest MIPROv2 demo axis if any hidden knowledge existed to teach.
- **Against:** This is the lens that refuted it. With the morphology printed in the gloss there is no latent knowledge left for a better prompt to surface, and the one hard rule is the most predictable rule in linguistics — refuted at major severity, no concrete rescue.

### Measurement skeptic
- **Supports:** Exact match on a rigid canonicalized template is a clean, unambiguous scoring surface with legible "expected X, got Y" diagnostics.
- **Against:** A clean scorer cannot save a task whose gap does not survive contact with a competent one-shot prompt; the measurement is precise but measures something the prompt can already do.

### Implementation lens (round 2)
- **Supports:** The oracle is a straightforward deterministic template filler and the morphology generator is conceptually simple to write from scratch.
- **Against:** No reusable repo exists — the one repo that touched the cluster (InductionBench, serving c23) has no case system, no glossing, and would be a from-scratch build; its I6 is zero and the implementation total is low. And ease is moot: the gate makes the candidate ineligible regardless of build cost.

### Research currency
- **Supports:** Rides live 2024-2026 lines — MTOB, LingOly and its follow-ups, ConLang generation, SIGMORPHON — earning a full tie-breaker.
- **Against:** The candidate inherits that currency rather than adding to it; standalone research novelty for a case-marked micro-language is low, and the currency does not offset the measurement refutation.

## Implementation: steps & risks

1. **Build the morphology generator from scratch.** No reusable code exists; write a seeded generator that samples roots plus a rotating inventory of case/number/tense affixes, a topic-fronting particle, and an allomorphy stratum, emitting a conlang sentence plus a tiny gloss table per instance.
2. **Write the deterministic template filler.** Implement the oracle that maps a parsed sentence to the rigid three-to-six-word English template (fixed articles, fixed slot order) and canonicalize for exact match.
3. **Attempt the gate rescue before any of the above ships.** The task is ineligible as designed; the only path to eligibility is the MTOB-style redesign that withholds affix semantics from the gloss so the morphology must be induced — but that is a different candidate and must be scoped and re-scored as one, not slipped in as a patch.

### Risks
- **One-shot collapse (the fatal one):** the gloss table hands over the morphology and case-beats-word-order is the highest-prior linguistic rule, so a single competent paragraph closes most of the gap and the incremental ladder disappears.
- **Rescue changes the task:** withholding affix semantics to restore headroom converts this into an induction task (a different candidate), so "fixing" it forfeits its identity rather than salvaging its score.
- **From-scratch build cost with no payoff:** the morphology, glossing, and allomorphy stratum must all be written new, and even a flawless build cannot lift a gated candidate off the floor.
