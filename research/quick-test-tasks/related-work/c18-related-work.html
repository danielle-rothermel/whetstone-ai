c18 Related Work: Depth-Controlled Synthetic Deduction (True/False/Unknown)

whetstone-ai · 2026-07-22

Seven 2023-2025 papers bound c18's design space, all synthetic depth-binned deduction with an automated ground-truth oracle. The closest living competitor is JustLogic (2025): a three-way True/False/Uncertain, single-shot, exact-match task that nearly co-locates with c18's own scoring design, meaning c18's task-shape novelty is thin. c18's real differentiator is a specific, currently-empty combination — fictional nonce surface + label-only exact match + a reseedable no-LLM generator + an independent forward-chaining oracle + a CSP stratum — that no single peer holds all of. The most robust cross-paper finding: single-pass accuracy declines monotonically with proof depth everywhere it is measured, and the collapse point is set by rule-set breadth, not depth alone — c18's plain if-then chaining sits in the easier, shallower-collapse regime.

Paper dossiers

PrOntoQA: models produce valid steps but cannot plan a proof, and no amount of sampling fixes it

2210.01240
Saparov & He (NYU) · ICLR 2023 (arXiv v4 2023-03-02)

Claim & novelty

Introduces PrOntoQA — the fictional-ontology generator behind c18's own codebase — whose chains-of-thought can be semantically parsed back into formal proof steps for step-level, not just label-level, grading. Novelty is combining semantically-parseable proofs with controlled true/false/fictional ontology, unary/binary predicates, and named deduction rules in one dataset (Table 1), letting the authors decompose "proof accuracy" into strictly-valid/broadly-valid/invalid and atomic/non-atomic/misleading categories no compared dataset (FOLIO, ProofWriter, GSM8K, SimpleLogic) supports simultaneously.

Protocol & observation (exact)

Single-shot: 8-shot in-context learning, greedy decoding — a fixed prompt of 8 fully-labeled demonstrations (context+query+CoT+label) followed by one held-out test question with the CoT and label stripped, generated in one forward pass. A separate self-consistency variant samples 40 completions at temperature 0.7 and votes — still single-shot per sample, aggregated post hoc. Output is a full chain-of-thought proof sequence plus a final True/False label, not the label alone, graded by a symbolic proof-checker (Algorithm 1) that reconstructs each sentence into a logical form.

Models & headline numbers

  • Only text-davinci-002 beats chance across the model-size sweep (350M → text-davinci-002).
  • 5-hop fictional-ontology proof steps: 93.2% strictly-valid, 2.4% broadly-valid, 5.9% invalid.
  • Accuracy falls to chance at 5-hop, top-down, fictional ontology — the paper's hardest configuration.
  • Self-consistency (0.56) and DFS-style demonstrations (0.55) are not significantly different from greedy CoT (0.545) at 5-hop — neither fixes proof planning.
  • Without distractors, InstructGPT predicts the label "almost perfectly" by string-matching the queried property — distractors are load-bearing, not optional.

Failure modes

  • Dominant failure is proof planning, not step invalidity: at branch points with multiple valid next steps, the model takes a strictly-valid but misleading one and rarely recovers.
  • Self-consistency sampling assigns higher probability to incorrect proofs than the correct gold proof on items the model gets wrong — beam search would not help either.
  • Real-world ("true") ontology accuracy does not degrade with depth, because pretrained knowledge lets the model "skip" hops — a confound c18's fictional surface is designed to block.

Corpus-claim verdict

Partially supported. The paper confirms fictional-ontology generation with contamination-resistant nonce concepts and fine-grained proof-step diagnosis, and confirms github.com/asaparov/prontoqa as the source of all generation code/data/outputs — both load-bearing parts of the corpus claim. correction The exact phrase "every wumpus is a yumpus" is a paraphrase of the paper's fictional-lexicon style (its actual examples use cat/carnivore/mammal and vumpus/numpus/dumpus/impus/wumpus/tumpus/zumpus/rompus/jompus), not a verbatim quote — a minor but real mismatch.

PrOntoQA-OOD: compositional generalization holds, but proof-by-cases and proof-by-contradiction need a worked demo

2305.15269
Saparov, Pang, Padmakumar, Joshi, Kazemi, Kim & He · NeurIPS 2023 (arXiv v3 2023-11-03)

Claim & novelty

Extends PrOntoQA to a complete natural-deduction rule set (all propositional connectives, not just modus ponens) with programmable depth, width, and compositionality, arguing that general deductive reasoning requires generalizing beyond the complexity level shown in demonstrations. Also contributes a formal CoT-correctness evaluator and the empirical finding that in-context generalization does not always favor same-distribution demonstrations — unexplained by existing ICL theories.

Protocol & observation (exact)

Single-shot: 8-shot (4-shot for depth/width sweeps) chain-of-thought prompting, one full CoT proof generated per instance, no interactive loop. Exact literal prompt template not reproduced in extraction — the worked examples are unreproduced figures; what is verified textually is that each instance has facts + a query + a gold CoT proof, fictional names throughout, and distractors forcing a choice between two candidate premises for the next step.

Models & headline numbers

  • Exact per-condition percentages missing from extraction — all quantitative results are reported only as figures (bar/line charts), not text or tables, in the source.
  • Qualitative headline: "in all but three experiments... the gap in proof accuracy between the ID and OOD settings is close to zero" — compositional generalization from single-rule demonstrations largely holds.
  • No model can perform disjunction elimination or proof-by-contradiction correctly without an in-context demonstration of that specific rule.
  • Model size does not strongly correlate with performance: LLaMA-65B performs comparably to PaLM-540B.
  • Depth generalization degrades for both ID and OOD settings as depth increases beyond the depth-2 training demonstrations.

Failure modes

  • GPT-3.5 acquires a distractor-copying heuristic when demonstrations lack distractors but the test item has them — copies irrelevant sentences into its output.
  • GPT-3.5 and PaLM both fail once compositional proofs mix 4 distinct rule types.
  • Removing distractors from demonstrations (while keeping them in the test item) leaves performance "largely unaffected... with the exception of a few rules for specific models."

Corpus-claim verdict

Partially supported. The paper genuinely supplies OOD depth-generalization and compositional-generalization evidence, matching the "depth/compositionality extension" framing. correction "Incremental-ladder" is a c18-side framing that does not map cleanly onto this paper's design: its depth/width test is a fixed-training-depth-2-vs-swept-test-depth comparison, not a many-rung ladder, and its central output is full-proof CoT graded by a formal verifier, not a per-depth label the way c18's own D0-D5 ladder is framed. Quantitative headroom magnitudes are themselves unverifiable from this local extraction (figures only).

FLD: a complete-axiom generator where even GPT-4 solves only half the problems, and depth collapses past 5-6

2308.07336
Morishita, Morio, Yamaguchi & Sogawa (Hitachi) · ICML 2023

Claim & novelty

Argues prior synthetic corpora (RuleTaker, AACorpus) use limited/arbitrary rule sets, capping the generalizability of any reasoning ability learned from them; builds FLD from the complete axioms of first-order predicate logic (Gödel completeness) instead. Even GPT-4 solves only about half of FLD's 10-shot problems by answer accuracy, and far less by the stricter step-checked proof accuracy.

Protocol & observation (exact)

Both modes tested. (1) A fine-tuned T5 "stepwise prover" generates one proof step at a time in a self-continuing loop until the hypothesis is (dis)proved — iterative but not interactive (no external feedback). (2) LLMs (GPT-4, GPT-3.5-Turbo, LongAlpaca-13B) get 10-shot in-context demonstrations of the full worked derivation format plus a CoT instruction, and generate the full proof-step sequence and final proved/disproved/unknown label in one pass.

Models & headline numbers

  • GPT-4, 10-shot: proof accuracy FLD 12.8 / FLD* 3.2; answer accuracy FLD 52.4 / FLD* 49.4 — "even GPT-4 can solve only half of the problems."
  • GPT-3.5-Turbo: proof accuracy FLD 0.0 / FLD* 2.0; answer accuracy FLD 35.8 / FLD* 37.6 — near the 33.3% random floor.
  • LongAlpaca-13B: answer accuracy FLD 21.2 / FLD* 19.6 — below the random-guess floor.
  • Fully fine-tuned T5 prover: FLD proof accuracy 75.8 (v2 corpus) vs. RuleTaker 92.4-93.9 — FLD is substantially harder despite equivalent supervision.
  • With the full axiom set (A≈10 rule choices/step), accuracy collapses to ~0% by depth 6-8 regardless of training depth; with implication-only rules (A≈2, the c18/RuleTaker regime) shallow-trained provers still generalize to depth 4-8.

Failure modes

  • Logical hallucination (conclusion not deducible from chosen facts), distractor confusion, and misinterpretation of negation (double-negation especially).
  • Under-use of "unknown": GPT-4 "seldom answered unknown... even if the given facts are not sufficient."
  • Answer/proof unfaithfulness: models "do not always faithfully follow the logical steps they themselves generate" — a correct label can hide an incorrect derivation.

Corpus-claim verdict

Supported. The paper explicitly grounds FLD in the Gödel completeness theorem, builds the generator from that axiom set, and every example carries a proved/disproved/unknown label under controllable depth (1-8) and distractors (up to ~20). No correction needed.

FLD×2 ("FLD*"): fine-tuning on principled synthetic logic lifts LLaMA-3.1-70B up to 30 points — but it never scores raw prompting accuracy

2411.12498
Morishita, Morio, Yamaguchi & Sogawa (Hitachi) · NeurIPS 2024 (arXiv v2 2024-12-23)

Claim & novelty

Proposes Additional Logic Training (ALT): fine-tuning LLMs on a scaled-up synthetic deduction corpus (FLD×2, ~100k WordNet vocabulary vs. ~15k in FLD, ~50 axioms+theorems vs. 13) built from four design principles (unknown facts, illogical/distractor samples, rule diversity, linguistic diversity). Shows ALT helps even very large pretrained models (LLaMA-3.1-70B, 15T+ tokens), not just small models as in prior synthetic-logic work.

Protocol & observation (exact)

This is a supervised fine-tuning + downstream-transfer study, not an in-context evaluation of the deduction task itself. Models are fine-tuned on FLD×2 samples for 1 epoch, then evaluated 5-shot (3-shot for BBH/AbductionRules) on 31 separate downstream benchmarks — the paper explicitly excludes its own synthetic corpus from evaluation ("training on them often leads to overfitting to their superficial and statistical cues"). No reported accuracy for a model directly answering a fresh FLD×2 instance via prompting alone — that number does not exist in this paper.

Models & headline numbers

  • LLaMA-3.1-8B, 5-shot average across 31 benchmarks: base 47.9 → +ALT-FLD×2 52.0 (best of 4 corpora tested; FLD 51.9, RuleTaker 50.1, PARARULE-Plus 48.1).
  • LLaMA-3.1-70B benchmark deltas after ALT-FLD×2: FOLIO 58.9→66.7, LogicNLI 34.9→50.9, RobustLR 49.6→81.6, AbductionRules 84.0→95.7.
  • Abstract: gains "up to 30 points on logical reasoning... up to 10 points on math and coding... 5 points on BBH."
  • Ablating any single design principle (DP1-DP4) from FLD×2 lowers the 8B average from 52.0 to between 50.6 and 51.4 — each principle contributes measurably.

Failure modes

  • ALT does not teach new facts, so pure knowledge-recall failures (e.g. ARC-challenge photosynthesis ordering) remain unimproved.
  • Without an explicit negative/insufficient-premise (illogical) training signal, models trained only on valid samples still hallucinate a conclusion even when premises are insufficient.
  • Omitting the Recall-Adam knowledge-forgetting-prevention step causes ALT-trained models to underperform the no-ALT baseline — fine-tuning on synthetic logic can displace pretrained factual knowledge.

Corpus-claim verdict

Partially supported, name correction. The paper is genuinely the intended follow-up to FLD, extending the same proved/disproved/unknown generator line under controllable depth (s=1-8) and distractors. correction The paper never uses the name "FLD*" anywhere in its text — its own name is "Formal Logic Deduction Diverse," stylized FLD×2 (FLD²). "FLD*" is a project-internal shorthand, not the authors' own naming; cite it as FLD×2.

Multi-LogiEval: 33 inference rules across three logic types, and non-monotonic reasoning gets easier with depth while classical logic collapses

2406.17169
Patel, Kulkarni, Parmar, Budhiraja, Nakamura, Varshney & Baral (Arizona State) · EMNLP 2024 Main

Claim & novelty

The first benchmark to jointly cover genuinely multi-step reasoning (depths 1-5, not single-step as in base PrOntoQA), three logic types together (propositional, first-order, non-monotonic), and a broad 33-rule inventory (60+ combinations across depths). LLMs show a significant, systematic accuracy drop as depth increases from 1 to 5 for classical logic; non-monotonic reasoning shows the opposite trend.

Protocol & observation (exact)

Single-shot: one <prompt, context, question> triplet per instance, zero-shot Chain-of-Thought, binary Yes/No label in one call. A secondary 3-shot variant and a two-stage neuro-symbolic pipeline (GPT-4 translates to a Prover9-executable program, then Prover9 solves) are also single-shot-per-stage, not interactive. Exact literal follow-on prompt string not reproduced in extraction; the natural-language story context plus yes/no entailment question structure is confirmed textually.

Models & headline numbers

  • Average accuracy across models drops from ~68% at depth-1 to ~43% at depth-5 for classical logic (PL/FOL combined).
  • PL average by depth: 85.42% (d1) → 42.96% (d5); FOL: 83.21% → 32.96%; NM (non-monotonic) average: 35.63% (d1) → 55.83% (d5) — increasing, not decreasing.
  • Several models perform below the computed per-depth random baseline (e.g. 86.63% at d1, 53.71% at d3) — systematic, not just noisy, failure.
  • Larger open-source models underperform smaller ones at high depth: Mistral-7B outperforms Orca-2-13B by "3x" at d5, despite being smaller.
  • 3-shot substantially raises accuracy at every depth (e.g. GPT-4 FOL d4 59.17%→68.33%) but "does not fully mitigate the inherent challenges... at higher depths" for open-source models.

Failure modes

  • Error propagation: mistakes in the initial reasoning step propagate through the chain, driving the d4-d5 collapse.
  • Wrong mapping of evidence from context/premises to conclusion (~27.4% of wrong d2 chains, ~22% of wrong d3 chains).
  • Longer reasoning chains do not correlate with correctness: ChatGPT generates the longest chains at d5 (13.85 avg.) yet is not the most accurate.

Corpus-claim verdict

Supported. Venue, depth range (1-5), 33 base inference rules, 60+ combinations, and the three logic types (PL/FOL/NM) are all confirmed directly against the paper's own text. No correction needed.

JustLogic: c18's closest living competitor — three-way exact match, depth 1-7, and only DeepSeek R1 beats the human average

2501.14851
Chen, Zhang & Tao · arXiv v2 2025-05-09; LaTeX source formatting (icml2025.sty, [accepted] option) suggests but does not independently confirm ICML 2025 acceptance

Claim & novelty

Argues prior deductive benchmarks lack task complexity, let prior world knowledge substitute for reasoning, or offer only superficial error analysis. JustLogic inserts real GenericsKB sentences into synthetically-derived propositional-logic argument structures with randomized truth values, combining synthetic datasets' argument complexity (infinite depth, 7 formal argument forms) with human-curated datasets' natural-language complexity. Most SOTA LLMs underperform the human average (73.0%); only DeepSeek R1 (80.9%) substantially exceeds it, and all models fall short of the 100% human ceiling.

Protocol & observation (exact)

Single-shot CQO→A: a context paragraph of premises, a fixed question ("Is the statement true, false, or uncertain?"), 3 answer options, one label out in one forward pass — identical in shape to c18's proposed design. Zero-shot, 3-shot, and CoT variants tested; the one genuinely multi-turn ablation is tree-of-thought (candidate-generation + candidate-evaluation prompts across up to depth+2 steps), a secondary probe, not the main protocol.

Models & headline numbers

  • DeepSeek R1: 80.9% (CoT) — best overall, only model to exceed the 73.0% human average; "moderate decline" at higher depths.
  • GPT-4o-mini: 0-shot 53.0%, few-shot 54.7% (best), CoT 51.8% — the rare case where CoT underperforms few-shot.
  • Llama3-8B: CoT 57.8% (best) vs. 0-shot 49.8%; Llama3-70B: CoT 64.6% vs. 0-shot 53.1%.
  • Prior-knowledge-independence test: JustLogic |Δ| = 0.4 (accuracy 33.7% vs. random 33.3%) — the most prior-knowledge-independent of 5 benchmarks tested, vs. FOLIO 6.7 and LogiQA 2.0's 27.1.
  • Tree-of-thought helped only GPT-4o (71.4% vs. 65.6% CoT) and hurt Llama3-8B (38.6% vs. 57.8% CoT) and GPT-4o-mini (48.6% vs. 51.8%).

Failure modes

  • Logical inconsistency: models generate arguments relying on premises that contradict their own earlier CoT steps.
  • Wrong application or wrong choice of argument form (e.g. invoking modus tollens where it cannot logically apply).
  • o1 answers "Uncertain" for 90% of depth=7 questions without faithfully engaging — suggestive of constrained test-time compute rather than a reasoning ceiling.

Corpus-claim verdict

Partially supported. The synthetic/programmatic generation, prior-knowledge independence testing, and fine-grained depth/argument-form error analysis are all directly confirmed as core author-stated contributions. correction ICML 2025 acceptance is inferred only from LaTeX formatting (icml2025.sty, [accepted] option) — "strongly implied but not independently confirmed by arXiv metadata" (no journal-ref present). Cite the venue as an arXiv 2025 preprint with implied-but-unconfirmed ICML 2025 acceptance, not as a settled fact.

SATBench: the hard tail from a different reasoning regime — even o4-mini is near-random on hard UNSAT puzzles

2505.14615
Wei, Wu, Wan, Suresh, Tan, Zhou, Koyejo, Wang & Aiken (Stanford, UCLA, UIUC, CMU, Nanjing) · arXiv v2 2025-09-22; EMNLP 2025 acceptance cited in some sources is not confirmable from arXiv metadata

Claim & novelty

2100 logical puzzles automatically generated from Boolean satisfiability (SAT/CNF) formulas, translated into natural-language scenarios and validated by solver-based and LLM-based consistency checks plus human spot-checks. Frames SAT as a search-based reasoning task (existence of a satisfying assignment, both SAT and UNSAT outcomes possible) distinct from inference-rule-chaining benchmarks (FOLIO, RuleTaker, PrOntoQA) — c18's own lineage.

Protocol & observation (exact)

Single-shot, 0-shot CoT: one call per puzzle (scenario + conditions + question), no demonstrations, model emits a reasoning trace plus a final [SAT]/[UNSAT] label; no external solver access permitted for the model under test. A separate single-shot judge call (GPT-4o) independently scores whether the reasoning trace is valid.

Models & headline numbers

  • o4-mini: overall 89.3% (highest), but UNSAT-Hard only 65.0% — "close to the random baseline of 50%."
  • Average across all 20 tested models on hard problems: 53.0% — "nearly equivalent to the random baseline."
  • SAT vs. UNSAT asymmetry on hard instances: 66.7% (SAT) vs. only 39.3% (UNSAT, below random).
  • Raw CNF/SAT-formula input beats natural-language narrative framing for every model tested (o4-mini 94.3% formula vs. 89.4% puzzle) — representation is a real, quantified difficulty dial.
  • Error-aware prompting (naming the 4 failure categories, still single-shot) corrects 60.4% (o4-mini) to 73.2% (DeepSeek-R1) of previously-failing cases — the single biggest single-shot prompt lever in this dossier set.

Failure modes

  • Satisfiability bias: models answer SAT but supply an invalid assignment, failing to check all clauses (68.7% of o4-mini's errors).
  • Context inconsistency: contradicting earlier reasoning steps within the same trace (44.7% of DeepSeek-R1's errors).
  • Condition omission and spurious commonsense priors not present in the stated constraints.

Corpus-claim verdict

Partially supported, venue flag. The SAT/CNF-to-puzzle generation, solver oracle, and documented large headroom on hard UNSAT (o4-mini near random) are all directly confirmed. Positioning: the family across three axes

The protocol axis (single-shot vs. interactive) is near-degenerate for this family: all seven papers are single-forward-pass at the model-under-test level. The only genuine multi-turn probes are JustLogic's tree-of-thought ablation and FLD's stepwise T5 prover — both secondary, and tree-of-thought mostly hurts. So the map below uses the three axes that actually separate these papers: what the verifier grades, content provenance, and the reasoning regime plus label space.

AxisPole APole BWhere c18 sits
A — Output shape / what the verifier grades Full proof-chain + step-level structural verifier: PrOntoQA, PrOntoQA-OOD, FLD, FLD×2 (all argue label-only accuracy is inadequate) Label-only, exact-match: JustLogic, Multi-LogiEval, SATBench (label track) Label-only exact-match pole. Deliberately drops full proof-chain output; its planned forward-chaining oracle re-derives the label but does not grade a model-emitted chain.
B — Content provenance / contamination resistance Fictional / nonce predicates ("wumpus/yumpus"): PrOntoQA, PrOntoQA-OOD. Content-free WordNet-templated: FLD, FLD×2 Real-world sentences, randomized truth: JustLogic. Naturalistic real-domain entities (less contamination-resistant): Multi-LogiEval. LLM-narrated CNF: SATBench Fictional / nonce pole, inherited directly from PrOntoQA.
C — Reasoning regime & difficulty dial Inference-rule chaining, depth = dial: PrOntoQA (hops), PrOntoQA-OOD (depth+width+composition), FLD/FLD×2 (depth 1-8 + rule-set size), Multi-LogiEval (depth 1-5 × 33 rules), JustLogic (depth 1-7 × 7 forms) Search / constraint satisfaction, clause-count = dial: SATBench (SAT/UNSAT existence) Core stratum: depth bins D0-D5 + distractor rules + CWA/negation-as-failure vs. open-world. The crowded cell is {rule-chaining × depth-dial × three-way label}; c18's empty cell is a specific combination, not a new shape

FLD, FLD×2, JustLogic, and c18's core stratum all live in the same densely-occupied cell. JustLogic is the closest living competitor and nearly co-locates with c18's scoring design (three-way True/False/Uncertain, single-shot, exact-match) — c18 cannot claim task-shape novelty over it. c18's distinguishing position is the specific triple {fictional surface + label-only exact match + reseedable-no-LLM generator} plus a planned independent forward-chaining oracle and a CSP stratum welded onto the deduction stratum — no single published peer holds all of these together. This is an integration/engineering contribution, not a new benchmark concept.

Capability synthesis

One-shot accuracy declines monotonically with depth everywhere; the collapse point depends on rule-set breadth, not depth alone

The single most robust cross-paper finding: accuracy falls as proof depth increases in every dossier that varies depth (Multi-LogiEval ~68%→~43% d1-d5; JustLogic monotonic decline through d7 and beyond; PrOntoQA falls to chance at 5-hop top-down; PrOntoQA-OOD degrades for both ID and OOD as depth grows). But the collapse point is not fixed — FLD's own argument is that with implication-only rules (A≈2, the PrOntoQA/RuleTaker regime c18 uses), shallow-trained provers still generalize to depth 4-8, while the full axiom set (A≈10) collapses to ~0% by depth 6-8 regardless of training depth. c18 sits in the easier, shallower-collapse regime. Failures are driven by planning and error propagation, not single-step invalidity: PrOntoQA's steps are 93.2% strictly-valid at 5 hops, yet the model picks a valid-but-misleading branch and rarely recovers; Multi-LogiEval shows mistakes in the initial step propagate through the chain, and longer reasoning chains do not correlate with correctness.

Interaction and extra demonstrations rarely help and can actively hurt; the reliable lever is CoT within one pass

The only genuine interactive ablation across the corpus — JustLogic's tree-of-thought — helped only GPT-4o and hurt Llama3-8B (57.8%→38.6%) and GPT-4o-mini; the authors conclude prompting techniques costlier than vanilla CoT offer little to no advantage. More demonstrations help modestly but saturate around 8-shot (PrOntoQA/PrOntoQA-OOD) and never remove the depth degradation (Multi-LogiEval 3-shot lifts every depth but "does not fully mitigate" high-depth failure); few-shot without a CoT rationale can even underperform zero-shot. Sampling/aggregation tricks do not fix planning either — PrOntoQA's self-consistency and DFS-demonstrations are statistically indistinguishable from greedy CoT, and probability aggregation actually favors wrong proofs on items the model gets wrong. The reliable single-shot lever is CoT plus naming the logic explicitly: JustLogic's CoT gains average +8 to +12 points over zero-shot; SATBench's error-aware prompting (naming failure categories, still single-shot) recovers 60-73% of previously failing cases, the single biggest swing measured anywhere in this dossier set.

Distractors are load-bearing, not optional: without them models label almost perfectly by shortcut-matching, not reasoning

PrOntoQA: "without any distractors, InstructGPT is able to predict the true/false label almost perfectly" by string-matching the queried property against context. PrOntoQA-OOD makes the identical design point. This is the single biggest risk to c18's ceiling: if the reseed-only baseline's regenerated instances lack strong distractors, even a naive prompt could score 85-95%+ by shortcut-matching rather than reasoning — whether the codebase's default settings emit distractors is the single biggest swing factor and must be checked directly in the harness.

Predicted floor and ceiling for the c18 reseed-only baseline

c18 config being scored: PrOntoQA instances regenerated as-is (fresh seeds, fresh fictional ontologies), existing hop-depth settings, native True/False exact-match output (the codebase's native closed-world 2-way label — no added Unknown handling or distractor/soft-rule strata beyond what the repo natively emits), cheap tier, temperature 0. No dossier tests this exact model tier, this exact 2-way native format, or PrOntoQA label-only accuracy for modern models — the closest label-only PrOntoQA numbers are proof accuracy on a 2022 model, so both ranges below carry real uncertainty. Four structural facts push the predicted score above the raw JustLogic/FLD proxies: native output is 2-way (50% floor, not 33%), c18 sits in the easy A≈2 if-then regime, its surface is clean fictional templates (easier than JustLogic's natural language or SATBench's narratives), and the 2026 cheap tier is stronger than the 2024 GPT-4o-mini/Llama3-8B proxies used throughout this literature.

Naive floor (0-shot)
~60–78%
Ceiling (CoT + logic-naming)
~75–90%

Uncertainty is moderate-to-high for the floor and moderate for the ceiling. Direction of every lever (CoT helps, distractors are required, depth degrades accuracy) is robust across papers; the magnitude for this exact 2026 cheap tier, this exact 2-way native format, is extrapolated from proxies rather than directly measured. Go/no-go gate: if the harness's default settings emit weak or no distractors, the naive floor risks landing at 85-95% via shortcut-matching, leaving little headroom — check the codebase's default distractor behavior before trusting the floor estimate. The native 2-way T/F format also omits the literature's most diagnostic failure mode (under-using "unknown" / open-world withholding), which argues for adding the Unknown axis if a strong model-discriminating signal is required beyond this reseed-only baseline.

Positioning & publishability synthesis

JustLogic already fills c18's near-identical cell; c18's added value there is narrow and must be stated honestly

JustLogic delivers a programmatically generated, depth 1-7, three-way True/False/Uncertain, exact-match, single-shot task with contamination control and depth laddering — structurally almost identical to c18's scoring design. c18's remaining differentiators against it are narrow: fully fictional/nonce surface vs. JustLogic's real-sentence randomized-truth surface (a trade, not a strict improvement — JustLogic even instruments its own NL complexity, which c18 does not); an explicit CWA-vs-open-world toggle as a named dial; the CSP stratum; and an independent forward-chaining oracle where JustLogic's failure analysis stays qualitative. Overlap here is high enough that c18 cannot claim task-shape novelty over this paper.

Against the FLD lineage, c18's cleanest non-overlapping contribution is a number that literature never reports

FLD×2 is upstream corpus-and-training methodology, not inference-time evaluation — it explicitly excludes its own corpus from evaluation and reports only downstream-transfer accuracy after fine-tuning. c18 would add exactly what FLD×2 never provides: a direct, off-the-shelf, frozen-model inference-time T/F/Unknown accuracy number on fresh depth-d instances. FLD itself does test LLM few-shot accuracy directly, but as a secondary analysis subordinate to its fine-tuned-prover study; c18's protocol treats that number as the primary deliverable.

If optimization yields large, verified gains, the defensible claim is optimizer-behavior on a contamination-free task — not a new benchmark or reasoning-science result

The planned un-optimized baseline is a faithful regeneration of PrOntoQA (fresh seeds, existing hop-depth settings, native 2-way T/F, no Unknown, no distractor/CSP additions beyond the codebase's native support) — not the full c18 design. Any first-round result is therefore a statement about prompt-optimization on PrOntoQA-style depth-binned deduction, not about c18's novel axes. The defensible workshop claim: automated prompt optimization (COPRO / MIPROv2 / GEPA) raises exact-match accuracy by a stated, CI-bounded margin over a strong fixed baseline (not a weak zero-shot strawman — JustLogic and Multi-LogiEval both show CoT/few-shot alone move accuracy substantially), with the gain broken out per depth and replicated under fresh-seed regeneration, since contamination-resistance is the whole rationale for the task.

The result belongs in prompt-optimizer benchmarking, not the deductive-reasoning-evaluation line

The deductive-reasoning-evaluation line's currency is about harder/cleaner benchmarks and reasoning-failure science (JustLogic and SATBench are its live 2025 entries); an un-optimized regeneration of a 2023 generator adds no benchmark novelty there, since JustLogic already fills the near-identical cell. The stronger fit is prompt-optimizer benchmarking (GEPA is an ICLR 2026 oral; MAS-PromptBench 2026 is a purpose-built optimizer-comparison benchmark) — a depth-parameterized, contamination-free, exact-match task with a tunable difficulty dial is exactly the clean testbed that line wants. The task is the instrument; the optimizers are the object of study.

Reviewers will demand a strong baseline, matched-budget optimizer comparison, per-depth breakdown, and fresh-seed replication; a main-conference benchmark slot is not realistic

Expected asks: a hand-written CoT/few-shot baseline (not a strawman), all three optimizers at matched rollout/token budget (GEPA's headline claim is efficiency), a per-depth accuracy breakdown, fresh-seed/held-out-ontology replication (the single most important ablation, given the task's whole rationale is contamination resistance), cross-model transfer, and floor/ceiling references with CIs. Not publishable: small or within-noise gains, gains that vanish on regeneration, "optimizer beats a weak zero-shot prompt," or ceiling/floor-saturated depths with no headroom. A main-conference deductive-reasoning-benchmark slot is not realistic since JustLogic/SATBench already own the 2025 benchmark frontier; workshop or short-paper is the honest ceiling.

Research-currency verdict: the generator lineage is alive, but the neighborhood is crowded with fresh 2025 entrants

OpenAlex citation counts, fetched 2026-07-22: PrOntoQA 39 (6 since 2025, still cited into 2026); PrOntoQA-OOD 17 (6 since 2025, a rising 2023:4→2024:7→2025:6 trend); FLD 2; FLD×2 2 (both since 2025); JustLogic 0; Multi-LogiEval 0; SATBench 1 (since 2026). The anchor generator c18 reuses is demonstrably current, not abandoned, supporting c18's "reuse a live codebase" premise. The low FLD-line counts are almost certainly undercounts for a known ICML/NeurIPS line, not a signal of low impact. The 0-1 counts for JustLogic, Multi-LogiEval, and SATBench are uninformative by construction (2024-2025 papers, indexing lag) — their existence as fresh entrants is itself the currency signal: the sub-field is producing new benchmarks right now. Net read: the family is alive but maturing, not surging, and is a crowded, well-trodden shape with 2025 entrants already occupying c18's near-neighborhood — arguing against pitching c18 as a novel benchmark and for using it as an instrument in the more decisively surging prompt-optimizer-benchmarking line instead. All OpenAlex counts undercount relative to Google Scholar.
